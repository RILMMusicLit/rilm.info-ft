Handwörterbuch der musikalischen Terminologie (2012)
  1. This publication should be converted from provided PDF files.
  2. General comments:
    • Page numbering begins anew from "1" in each article.
    • German-style low-high quotation marks are used, both double („ ‟) and single (‚ ‛) Replace all with <q> tags; use @rend="single" for the single quotes. Keep apostrophes (’) as Unicode characters.
    • Emphatic text includes text in small caps and "sperren", or loose letter spacing.
    • Italic type is never used for emphasis. It is used either for
      1. Titles, or
      2. Cross references, which are indicated by right-pointing arrows preceding the italic text.
  3. All entries are <text type="topic">.
  4. All entries have a <div type="heading">, <div type="body">, and <div type="ography">, which should all be at the same structural level.
    • Each <div type="body"> will have several <div type="section"> elements nested in it.
    • Make sure that sections are properly nested: Roman numerals, then Arabic numerals, then letters:
      I. Highest level.
      (1) Second level.
      (a) Lowest level.
  5. Entries begin with an introductory paragraph or two, then have a summary of the article that mirrors the structure of the article's sections and their numbering, but without line breaks. If you can identify this, tag as a <div type="summary">.
  6. Bibliographies are marked by the head "Lit.:".
    • Items in the bibliography are usually separated by semicolons. Omit the semicolons after separating into separate <item><bibl> elements.
    • Author names are given in small caps.
    • Titles, regardless of whether article, book, etc., are in Roman type. Article and collection articles have a comma between.
    • If the item is a monograph, or part of a monograph, publication place and date follow the last comma and precede the semicolon
