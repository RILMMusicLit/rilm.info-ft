Encyclopedia of French Art Song

An encyclopedia, in English, of works, persons, topics, genres, and instruments. To be converted from a provided PDF file.

    • Abbreviations; tag as a two-column table. Note that the abbreviations are lower case in the source; please capitalize.
    • Acknowledgements
  2. General notes on articles:
    • Article heads are followed by a tabular space.
    • Tag all italic text (not in the head) with <hi rend="italic">
    • Tag all bold text (not in the head) with <ref>. Bold text always indicates a cross-reference. No need to tag with <hi rend="bold">.
    • If a text is both bold and italic (Cinq Mélodies de Venise), tag <hi> inside <ref>:
      <ref target="fas00000"><hi rend="italic">Cinq Mélodies de Venise</hi></ref>
  3. Articleson works:
    • Articles on works have a heading in bold-italic (A Clymène). Use the heading as //titleStmt/title in the TEI header, and in <div type="heading"> in the TEI body, as shown in the example files.
    • Tag quoted poetry in a <cit rend="block"> with <lg> and <l>. Tag other indented quotes with <cit rend="block"> and <p>.
  4. Person articles have a heading in bold, capitalized, followed by "(B ", indicating birth info.
    • Use the heading text in the //titleStmt/title in the TEI header and in <div type="heading">. OMIT any variants in parenthesis from the header, but do include them in the heading div.
    • Tag birth / death dates and places if given.
    • Some longer person articles have an in-line worklist at the end, indicated by "Poetry: ". Start a <div type="works">, use "Poetry:" as a <head>, and tag the titles and dates in <list><item><bibl><title> as in fas-example3.xml.
  5. Topical articles have a heading in bold, followed by "(FR " or rarely some other language identifier: "(SCOT", "(BQ", "(SP", etc. Choose <text @type="topic"> except in other cases below. Note: If the article heading begins with a lower-case French article "le", "la", or "l’", OMIT the "l" article from the //titleStmt/title in the TEI header, but include it in the heading div. See example files 5, 6, and 7.
    • If the first sentence of an article contains the word "instrument", the text @type should be "instrument".
    • If the article title begins with a capital letter, but there is neither (B nor language designation following--in other words, no opening parenthesis--then set text @type to "matter" and RILM will sort them out.
  6. Back matter consists only of (1) About the Author and (2) Colophon.
