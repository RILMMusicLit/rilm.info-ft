  • General notes:
    • This work consists of four parts (Teil), each of which includes 2 or 3 volumes (Band):
      1. Titlekatalog: a catalogue of opera titles and their composer(s), 3 vols.
      2. Komponisten: a catalogue of composers of opera with lists of titles composed by each, 3 vols.
      3. Librettisten: a catalogue of authors of opera texts with lists of titles written by each, 3 vols.
      4. Nachträge: supplements to the prior parts--chronologies, indexes, and additional entries.
      The first three parts are similar and will be processed similarly, but there are differences in the tagging details.
    • Each volume (11 total) begins with a half-title page, a series-title page, a volume-title page, and a copyright page. There is no need to scan or convert the half-title or series-title pages.
    • Each volume has a distinct publication year and ISBN number, which should be reflected in the TEI headers.
  • Part I: Titel katalog
    • The front matter of Band 1 consists of an introduction given in four languages (German, English, French, and Italian) and a list of abbreviations ("Abkürzungen").
      • Create a separate TEI file for each translation of the introduction--"Zur Einführung", "Introduction", "Introduction", and "Presentazione".
        • Start a new <div type="section"> at each center-aligned heading ("The Opera", "Bibliographical and Source Works on Opera", etc.)
        • Tag footnotes as described in http://rilm.info/ft/tei_rilm_instructions.html#footnotes.
        • Tag the bibliography in the middle of the second section as a <list>; keep the City names outside the <bibl> elements:
          <list><item>Berlin: <bibl><author><forename>HERBERT</forename> <surname>GRAF</surname></author>, <title>Das Repertoire[…]
      • Tag the list of abbreviations as a two-column table. The letters along the left ("A", "B", etc.) may be omitted.
    • The body of Part I is a tabular list of opera titles with additional data for each.
      • Create a TEI file for each distinct title. For example, on page 1 of vol. 1, only one file each should be created for the titles "À la belle étoile" and "À la mer". These are indicated by ditto (") marks or by exact repetition of the title text.
      • Printed column 1--use for the <title> element in the TEI header, and as a <head><title type="work> in the <div type="body">. Transpose the articles in parentheses ("The", "La", "L' ", etc.) to the beginning of the title.
      • Create a five-column <table> for the remaining four printed columns (printed column 2 is to be split in two columns in the TEI table). Spell out any repetitions indicated by (") or by (-); If a space is blank in the print, leave an empty <cell> element.
        • Insert a row for column headings with the contents "[Gattung des Werkes]", "[Anzahl der Akte"], "[Komponist(en)]", "[Textautor(en)]", and "[Nähere Aufführungsdaten]". Include the square brackets.
        • First <cell>: use the text from printed column 2 ("Optte", "Vaudeville", "Op.ser.", etc.) and tag <genre>.
        • Second <cell>: use the numeral from printed column 2 (1, 1, 3, etc.) and tag <biblScope unit="acts">
        • Third <cell>: use the text in printed column 3. Tag with <rilmPerson><author> and <forename> and <surname> where possible. Multiple names may be separated in print by "u.", "m.", "v." or commas; tag each separately and leave any words not capitalized outside the <rilmPerson> elements. Enclose each in a <ref> pointing to the name entry in any of the other parts (composers, librettists, supplement).
        • Fourth <cell>: use the text in printed column 4. Tag with <rilmPerson><author role="librettist"> and <forename> and <surname> where possible. Multiple names are separated in print by "u." (and) or "m." (with) or commas. Enclose in a <ref> pointing to the name entry in any of the other parts (composers, librettists, supplement).
        • Fifth <cell>: use the text in printed column 5. Tag as <rilmPerformance type="first">. Tag text before the date as <placeName>, the date as <date>, and text following the date as <orgName>. Printed dates are in D.M.YYYY format.
    • There is a brief catalogue silently inserted between the ("E") and ("F") sections on pp. 367-368 of volume 1, entitled "Bühnenwerke mit Musik, deren Texte Cervantes Roman....zur Grundlage haben."
      • Use the entire title as the <title> in the TEI header and as the <div type="heading">
      • Omit the text "Komponist (….….….…...)" at the top right.
      • Set as a simple <list>.
  • Part II: Komponisten will be, for the most part, treated similarly to Part I.
    • Front matter: tag as for Part I; use the printed titles ("Vorwort", "Preface", etc.)

    • The entries in the main body are all persons. Nearly all include a heading with a personal name and birth and death information, followed by a list of works.

      Do not create a TEI file for entries that have only see references (indicated by "s."). IF the target entries can be found, add the name to the target documents as described here.

      IF the target entry cannot be found (often it cannot), OR if the reference says only "s. Index" or "s. Nachtrag", OMIT the reference.

      • Headings should be tagged as <div type="heading"><p> containing a <rilmPerson> and <persName>:
        • Tag the letterspaced, underlined name as <surname>; close up the spaces.
        • Tag capitalized names in normal spacing, not underlined, as <forename>; non-capitalized words e.g. "dall' " as <nameLink>
        • Insert a comma between the surname and forename(s) and use for the TEI <title>.
        • The information in parentheses should be tagged with <birth>, <death>, <date> and <placeName> as appropriate. Dates are printed as D.M.YYYY; explanatory phrases suce as "54 Jahre alt" should be included within the <rilmPerson> element, but left outside of <birth> or <death> tags.
        • Some entries with long lists have the heading name repeated midway through the list; These are distiguished from true headings in that they are NOT letter spaced. (See page 126 of Part II, Vol. 1, "Boieldieu, F.A." for an example). OMIT these.
      • Very few entries have a one-line body text immediately following the entry heading. tag as a <div type="body">

        [A r t u s Alexandre (gest. 21.8.1911 La Varenne bei Paris, 90 Jahre alt)
        Komponist von mehr als 250 Melodramen un. Feerien]
         
        [C a f a r o Pasquale (8.2.1706 (1726?) S. Pietro/Galatino - 23.10.1787)
        (trat 1735 als Schüler ins Conservat. de la Pietra ein, das Geburtsjahr dürfte daher wohl 1726 sein)]
         

        These would be tagged as follows:
        <head><rilmPerson><persName><surname>Artus</surname> <forename>Alexandre</forename></persName> (<death>gest. <date when="1911-08-21 when-custom="1911">21.8.1911</date> <placeName>La Varenne bei Paris</placeName></death>, 90 Jahre alt)</rilmPerson></p></div>
        <div type="body"><p>Komponist von mehr als 250 Melodramen un. Feerien</p></div>
        <div type="heading"><p><rilmPerson><persName><surname>Cafaro</surname> <forename>Pasquale</forename>(<birth><date when="1706-02-08" when-custom="1706">8.2.1706 (<date when="1726" precision="low">1726</date>?) <placeName>S. Pietro/Galatino</placeName></birth> - <death><date when="1787-10-23">23.10.1787</date></death>)</rilmPerson></p></div>
        <div type="body"><p>(trat 1735 als Schüler ins Conservat. de la Pietra ein, das Geburtsjahr dürfte daher wohl 1726 sein)</p></div>
      • The work lists are printed in table with four-columns. Enclose in a <div type="works"> and tag as a five-column <table> as described below. If there is a subheading such as "Possen" or "Op.:" aligned with the main heading, tag it as <head>; if an additional heading is encountered (that is not a repeat of the composer name), end the <table> element, tag the heading <head>, and start a new <table> after.
        • Insert a row at the top of each table for column headings, with the contents "[Titel des Werkes]", "[Gattung des Werkes]", "[Anzahl der Akte]", "[Textautor(en)]", and "[Nähere Aufführungsdaten]" in italics. Include the square brackets.
        • First <cell>: use the text from printed column 1. Tag the text as <title type="work">. If the first column begins with a numeral and period, open the <title> element after it.
        • Second <cell>: use the text in printed column 2 as <genre>
        • Third <cell>: use the numeral in printed column 2; tag with <biblScope type="acts">
        • Fourth <cell>: use the text in printed column 3.
          • Multiple names may be separated in print by "u.", "m.", "v." or commas; tag each separately and leave any words not capitalized outside the <rilmPerson> elements.
          • Tag names without parentheses with <rilmPerson><author role="librettist">, and <forename> and <surname> where possible; tag names inside parentheses as <rilmPerson><persName>, and <forename> and <surname> where possible.
          • Enclose in a <ref> pointing to the name entry in the other parts (librettists, supplement).
          • Do not tag if the column begins "Vater", "Sohn", or "nach".
        • Fifth <cell>: use the text in printed column 4. Tag as <rilmPerformance type="first">. Tag text before the date as <placeName>, the date as <date>, and text following the date as <orgName>. Printed dates are in D.M.YYYY format.

        • Fourth <cell>: use the text in printed column 3.

          • Leave the "m. " outside of the <rilmPerson> element.
          • Multiple names may be separated in print by "u.", "m.", "v." or commas; tag each separately and leave any words not capitalized outside the <rilmPerson> elements.
          • Enclose each in a <ref> pointing to the name entry in this or other parts
        • Fifth <cell>: use the text in printed column 4.
          • Omit the parentheses.
          • Tag as <rilmPerson><persName>, and <forename> and <surname> where possible.
          • Multiple names may be separated in print by "u.", "m.", "v." or commas; tag each separately and leave any words not capitalized outside the <rilmPerson> elements.
          • Enclose each in a <ref> pointing to the name entry in the other parts (composers, supplement).
          • Do not tag if the text begins "Musik" or "Diverse".
        • Sixth <cell>: use the text in printed column 5. Tag as <rilmPerformance type="first">. Tag text as <placeName> and the date as <date>. Printed dates are in D.M.YYYY format. (There are no <orgName>s in this part.)
