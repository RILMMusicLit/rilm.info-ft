Dizionario Enciclopedico Universale della Musica e dei Musicisti [DEUMM] part 2 - Lessico

[*Last Revised 4/20/2022*.]

The second stage of RILMs conversion of DEUMM will be the 4-volume Lessico, an encyclopedia of terms and places. You will produce one PDF for each of the volumes (scanned at 300 dpi, grayscale for text-only pages, full color for plates and other color illustrations, and OCR text in Italian), plus article-level TEI files.

1. General principles:
  • Each volume of the Lessico has a different year and ISBN, given on the copyright page of each. The correct values should be included in the TEI header of each article.
  • xml:id's: DEUMM online will be a continually updated resource, so each <div>, <p>, and <bibl> element must have a unique identifier: an xml:id element containing "id-" followed by a UUID. For example: <p xml:id="id-f11a99bb-5a84-12df-f1b2-b9374b7da8ab">. The following six elements require an @xml:id :
    – <bibl>, <div>, <docAuthor>, <figure>, <p>, <rilmPerson>, <rilmPerformance>
  • Abbreviation of terms within articles: Every mention of the term in the title of the article is abbreviated to its first letter, capitalized. Such abbreviations should be expanded to the full term, uncapitalized except in the case of place names. For example, in the article "Logaritmo", the second sentence begins:
    [Il L. acustico ne è un particolare tipo,… ]
    The "L." should be expanded to "logaritmo":
    Il <hi rend="italic"><seg type="exp">logaritmo</seg> acustico</hi> ne è un particolare tipo,…
    Please tag these expanded abbreviations as <seg type="exp">.
  • General abbreviations (provided as DEUMM-Lessico-abbreviations.txt by RILM ): Where these abbreviations are used in the articles and bibliographies, they should be expanded to the full word or phrase.
  • Identification of terms with IDs from RILMs database of terms: RILM will provide a list of terms along with integer topic_IDs, and category of term. If an unambiguous match can be made between the article title (in un-inverted form) and a <term> in DEUMM-lessico-terms-ids.xml:
    • The <text> @type value should be gotten from the <category> value in the list.
    • The <term> tag applied to the article title should have an @xml:id attribute and a @topicID attribute that contains the ID.
    • If a name cannot be matched with those in the list provided by RILM, include an term @topicID attribute (topicID=""). Many terms will not have a @topicID match.
  • Typography: render all single quotes and apostrophes as ’ (hex2019); double quotes (« ») as <q> tags (except in bibliographies; see below).
  • Typography: normalize case where small caps are used in the print (particularly in bibliographies).
  • Typography: superscript "o" used as an ordinal (as in "50º" or "50, meaning "50th") should be encoded as hex00ba ("masculine ordinal"), not as hex00b0 ("degree sign"), nor as a <hi> element. Superscript "a" used as an ordinal ("100ª") should be encoded as hex00aa ("feminine ordinal").
2. Front matter:
  • Front matter of Lessico vol. 1 consists of the following:
    • [Title page]
    • [Copyright page]
    • Prefazione
    • Avverteze per la consultazione
    • Abbreviazione
    • Elenco dei Collaboratori del primo volume
    Omit the half-title page.
  • For volumes 2-4, capture only
    • [Copyright page]
    • Elenco dei Collaboratori del [secondo, terzo, quarto] volume
3. Short- to Medium-length articles:

1-6 paragraphs of body text, followed by bibliographies in paragraph form.

  • Capture the bold article heading as the <title> in the <teiHeader>.
  • Begin the <body> element with a <div type="subheading">. Tag the article title as <term>, and add an @topicID attribute as described above. Add an @xml:lang="it" attribute.
    [Ad libitum. Locuzione indicante libertà interpretativa:...]
    <div type="subheading"><p><hi rend="bold"><term topicID="" xml:lang="it">Ad libitum</term>.</hi> Locuzione indicante libertà interpretativa:...</p>...</div>
  • If the bold article heading is followed by terms in italic (or Greek), within parentheses, mark each italic (or Greek) term with <term>, adding its own @topicID and @xml:lang, and a @corresp attribute:
    [A due (fr. à deux). Expressione...]
    [Adufe (dall’arabo duff). Tamburello...]
    <p><hi rend="bold"><term topicID="" xml:id="id-123" xml:lang="it">A due</term></hi> (fr. <hi rend="italic"><term topicID="" xml:id="id-124" corresp="#id-123" xml:lang="fr">à deux</term></hi>). Expressione...</p>
    <p><hi rend="bold"><term topicID="" xml:id="id-234" xml:lang="it">Adufe</term></hi> (dall’arabo <hi rend="italic"><term topicID="" xml:id="id-235" corresp="#id-234" xml:lang="ar">duff</term></hi>). Tamburello...</p>
  • If the bold article heading is followed by a numeral, and another numeral in bold appears later in the article, it indicates there is more than one definition of the term.
    • Start a new <p> element at each numbered definition.
    • Mark any parenthetical terms after subsequent numbers as <term>, without @corresp link to the first term, as shown here:
    [Adonio. - 1) (ἀδώνιος; lat. adonius versus) Termine di metrica greca e latina che indica un verso composto di un dattilo e di un trocheo (–́⏑⏑, –⏑^); ricorre nel ­sistema saffico minore (→ SAFFICO, SISTEMA). Il nome de­riva dal fatto che era impiegato nelle cantilene rituali del culto di Adone (→ ADONIDIA). - 2) (ἀδώνιον) Ge­nere di antica marcia militare suonata sull’aulos, tipica della Laconia.]
    <p><hi rend="bold"><term topicID="" xml:id="id-345" xml:lang="it">Adonio</term>. - 1)</hi> (<term corresp="#id-345" topicID="" xml:id="id-346" xml:lang="gr">ἀδώνιος</term>; lat. <hi rend="italic"><term corresp="#id-345" topicID="" xml:id="id-347" xml:lang="la">adonius versus</term></hi>) Termine di metrica greca e latina che indica un verso composto di un dattilo e di un trocheo (–́⏑⏑, –⏑^); ricorre nel ­sistema saffico minore (<ref target="na">Saffico, Sistema</ref>). Il nome de­riva dal fatto che era impiegato nelle cantilene rituali del culto di Adone (<ref target="deu70000">Adonidia</ref>).</p>
    <p><hi rend="bold">- 2)</hi> (<term topicID="" xml:id="id-348" xml:lang="gr">ἀδώνιον</term>) Ge­nere di antica marcia militare suonata sull’aulos, tipica della Laconia.</p>
5. Long articles

Long articles have a "SOMMARIO" section following the article heading. The Sommario section can be omitted, as they will be automatically generated from the <head> elements of the various <div type="section">s.

  • Take care to nest sections properly. Note that in DEUMM, roman and common numerals, as well as letters, are used indiscriminately to number sections. Sometimes there is no numbering at all. Include the section number (or letter), if present, as @n attribute of both the <div> and <head> elements.
6. Special bibliographical articles

A number of articles have lengthy lists besides a "normal" Bibliography. Please tag the following with <text> @type="special", as they will need to be revisited later.

  • Biblioteche musicali (v.1, p.320 ff): Note that many items in the lists at the end have their own bibliographies.
  • Dizionari (v.2, p.53 ff): Start a <div type="ography"> at the heading "a) Dizionari lessicali e terminologici", and at each of the headings b through o. Start a final one for the "Bibl." on p.60.
  • Monumenti musicali (v.3, p.182 ff): Tag the lists as <list>. There are some nested lists, such as "Part Music" (pp.186-87)
  • Musicologia (v.3, p.281 ff): Similar to "Biblioteche" article, where items in a list have individual bibliographies.
  • Periodici musicali (v.3, p.590 ff): Nesting and sections should be clear from the print
  • Repertori bibliografici
  • Societ
7. <docAuthor>s and editors

Many articles will have an author given after the end of the text, justified right in small caps. Make a <div type="authors"> for this, and wrap the author's name in <docAuthor> and <persName> with tags for <forename> and <surname>.

<div type="authors"><persName><forename>Paulheinz</forename> <surname>Beck</surname></persName></div>

Frequently, a translator's name will also be given, on the same line, justified left in normal case, in parens, preceded by "trad.":
[(trad. Vera Bertolino)]
Place these in the same <div type="authors">, but in a separate <docAuthor> element. Add an @type attribute with value "translator" to the <docAuthor>.

<docAuthor role="translator">(trad. <persName><forename>Vera</forename> <surname>Bertolino</surname></persName>)</docAuthor>
  • NB: Although the translator's name appears first in the print, reading left to right, include it in the TEI after the true author.
  • In rare cases, there will be more than one true author in small caps. Again, tag the two authors' names in the same <div> but separate <docAuthor>s.
  • In rarer cases, there may be two true authors and a translator.

See example file deu70005.xml

7. See-references

An article heading followed by a right-pointing arrow (→), followed by text in small caps, indicates a see-reference:
[De Ana, Francesco → ANA, FRANCESCO D’.]

  • If there is no other information given, do not make an XML file or PDF for these, but add them to <keywords> element of the target article as described at http://rilm.info/ft/tei_rilm_instructions.html#see-refs
  • If translations of the term are given in parentheses, before the arrow, include those translations in the <keywords> element of the target article:
    Adonia (ἀδώνια) → ADONIDIA.
    Adoniasmo (ἀδωνιασμός) → ADONIDIA.
    <textClass>
    <keywords>
    <term>adonia</term>
    <term>ἀδώνια</term>
    <term>adoniasmo</term>
    <term>ἀδωνιασμός</term>
    </keywords>
    </textClass>
  • If any language is given in the reference, include it in the <term> @xml:lang attribute inside <keywords>:
    Armonie (anche harmonie, antico fr.) → GHIRONDA.
    <textClass>
    <keywords>
    <term>armonie</term>
    <term xml:lang="fr">harmonie</term>
    </keywords>
    </textClass>
  • If a number or letter is given following the target term, the reference is to a specific section of an article. Nevertheless, treat as above, by adding to the <keywords> element of the target article.
8. Cross-references
  • A right-pointing arrow (→) or a wavy right-pointing arrow (⤳) within the body text indicates a cross reference, or see-also reference.
  • A straight arrow indicates a target within the same part of the encyclopedia—from Lessico to Lessico, for example.
  • A wavy arrow indicates a target in a different part of the encyclopedia—from Lessico to Biografie, for example.
  • They might point to a whole article, or to a section within an article:
    (→ DULCE MELOS) or (⤳ HOMER, 2)
    The first points to the whole "Dulce Melos" article in the Lessico part of the encyclopedia. The second points to person number 2 in the family article on "Homer" in the Biografie part.
  • The text "cfr. §" indicates a reference to another section within the same article: (cfr. § Musica vocale, a). The text following "§" gives the <head> and head:@n of the <div> that is targeted. There are examples in the file deu00004.xml.

Tag a reference to a whole article as a <ref> element with @target containing the value of the @xml:id of the <text> element of the target article. Tag a reference to a section with @target containing the concatenated @xml:id of the <text> target, "#", and the @xml:id of the <div>:

<ref target="deu00000">Dulce Melos</ref>
<ref target="deu11111#id-9b05e5d0-5cf9-11ec-bf63-0242ac130002">Homer, 2</ref>

NB. Omit the arrow characters from the TEI; the link created by the <ref> tag will mark the reference.

9. Tavole (photograph plates)

The indication "Tav." in the page margin next to the article title refers to separately printed illustrations on plates inserted elsewhere in the volume. The numbers given indicated the relevant plate number(s): Tav. 5-6.

  • Captions for the illustrations for a given article appear all together before the illustrations, with the article title in bold type.
  • The captions are numbered with bold numerals. These refer to the small numbers printed at the lower right or left of each illustration.
  • Credits for the illustrations are given in smaller type after the abbreviation "Fot.:" (photos). The credits are also numbered in reference to the illustrations.
  • Make a <div type="section"> with the <head> "Tavole" before the <div type="ography">. All the color illustrations should be included in this <div>.
  • Make a <figure> element for each illustration. This element should contain:
    1. a <p> element containing the caption
    2. a <graphic> element containing the image file name as a @url attribute
    3. a <p> element containing the credit
    This is demonstrated in example file deu80001.xml.
