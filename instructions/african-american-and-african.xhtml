Biographical Dictionary of Afro-American and African Musicians
  1. General comments:
    • This publication should be converted from scanned print
    • Articles will have <div type="body">, and one or two <div type="ography>.
    • American-style double quotation marks are used.
    • Italic type is used to indicate titles, so tag with <title> rather than <hi>.
  2. Front matter includes the Title page, copyright page, Contents, Preface, Guide to Use, and List of Abbreviations.
    • Set the List of Abbreviations as a <table>.
  3. Person give the personal name given in surname, forename order, in all bold, all-capitals and ending with a period. Next, the person's occupation(s) is (are) given followed by birth and death date information, as available, in parentheses. Tag up to the first period of the first <p> as a <rilmPerson> element.
    • [ADDERLEY, NATHANIEL (“NAT”). Jazz cornetist/Trumpeter (b. 25 November 1931 in Tampa, Florida).]
    <p><rilmPerson><persName><surname>ADDERLEY</surname>, <forename>NATHANIEL</forename> (“<forename type="alt">NAT</forename>”)</persName>. <occupation>Jazz cornetist/Trumpeter</occupation>, (<birth><date when="1931-11-25" when-custom="1931">25 November 1931/date> in <placeName>Tampa, Florida</placeName>)</rilmPerson>. ...</p>
  4. Bibliographies are headed by the abbreviation "BIBL".
    • Individual items in the bibliographies are delimited by periods: [BIBL: Charles Battle, Negroes on the Island of Rhode Island (Newport, Rhode Island, 1932). Howard Brooks, The Negro in Newport (Newport, 1946).]
      <div type="ography">
      <head>BIBL:</head>
      <list>
      <item><bibl><author><forename>Charles</forename> <surname>Battle</surname></author>, <title>Negroes on the Island of Rhode Island</title> (<pubPlace>Newport, Rhode Island</pubPlace>, <date>1932</date>).</bibl></item>

      <item><bibl><author><forename>Howard</forename> <surname>Brooks</surname></author>, <title>The Negro in Newport</title> (<pubPlace>Newport</pubPlace>, <date>1946</date>).</bibl></item>
      </list>
      </div>
    • Items that are abbreviated are keyed to the list of abbreviations at the back of the book. I have created a text file "BAA-bibl-replace.txt", placed in Google Docs, that lists the abbreviations and their full versions. Please replace the abbreviations in the bibliographies with a <bibl> element containing the full version from the list:
      [BIBL: BPIM 4 (July 1976). SouRBM.]
      <div type="ography">
      <head>BIBL:</head>
      <list>
      <item><bibl><title type="periodical">The Black Perspective in Music</title> <biblScope unit="issue">4</biblScope> (<date>July 1976</date>)</bibl></item>
      <item><bibl><editor><forename>Eileen</forename> <surname>Southern</surname></editor>, ed., <title>Readings in Black American Music</title> (<pubPlace>New York</pubPlace>: <publisher>W. W. Norton</publisher>, <date>1971</date>)</bibl></item>
      </list>
      </div>
  5. Discographies are headed "DISC" in print. They should be treated in the same way as bibliographies, that is, abbreviations should be expanded. The same table of abbreviations applies.
  6. Cross references within articles are marked by asterisks. The text before the asterisk is the title of the target entry.
  7. See references are in the form [NAME, optional other text. See NAME]"
  8. Back matter includes several Appendices in the form of lists, a general Bibliography, and an Index. Set all as <list>s except the index, which should be treated like other back-of-book indexes.
