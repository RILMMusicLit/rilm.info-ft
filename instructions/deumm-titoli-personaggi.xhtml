Dizionario Enciclopedico Universale della Musica e dei Musicisti [DEUMM] part 3 - I Titoli e i Personaggi

[*Last Revised 7/20/2022*.]

The third stage of RILMs conversion of DEUMM will be the 3-volume I Titoli e i Personaggi, an encyclopedia of musical works by title and historical or fictional personal names. You will produce article-level TEI files.

1. General principles:

  • Each volume of the I Titoli e i Personaggi has a different year and ISBN, given on the copyright page of each. The correct values should be included in the TEI header of each article.
  • xml:id's: DEUMM online will be a continually updated resource, so each <div>, <p>, and <bibl> element must have a unique identifier: an @xml:id attribute containing "id-" followed by a UUID. For example: <p xml:id="id-f11a99bb-5a84-12df-f1b2-b9374b7da8ab">. The following six elements require an @xml:id :
    – <bibl>, <div>, <docAuthor>, <figure>, <p>, <rilmPerson>, <rilmPerformance>
  • General abbreviations (provided as DEUMM-Lessico-abbreviations.txt by RILM): Where these abbreviations are used in the articles and bibliographies, they should be expanded to the full word or phrase.
  • Typography: render all single quotes and apostrophes as ’ (hex2019); double quotes (« ») as <q> tags).
  • Typography: normalize case where small caps are used in the print (particularly in bibliographies).
  • Typography: superscript "o" used as an ordinal (as in "50º" or "50, meaning "50th") should be encoded as hex00ba ("masculine ordinal"), not as hex00b0 ("degree sign"), nor as a <hi> element. Superscript "a" used as an ordinal ("100ª") should be encoded as hex00aa ("feminine ordinal").

2. Front matter:

  • Front matter of Lessico vol. 1 consists of the following:
    • [Title page]
    • [Copyright page]
    • Prefazione
    • Abbreviazioni
    • Avvertenza (at the bottom of the page)
    Capture these in one xml file, <text type="matter">
  • For volumes 2-3, capture only the [Copyright page]
Articles types:

There are three kinds of articles in this part of DEUMM:

  1. Articles on a single work
  2. Articles containing a list of works
  3. Articles about a fictional or historical person with a following list of works.
Articles on a single work (example files deu30001, deu30002):
  • <text> @type is "work"
  • Use the bold text as the <title> in the TEI header. Begin the <text> with a <div type="subheading"> and begin the first <p> with a <bibl> element. Tag the bold text as <hi rend="bold"> and <title>.
  • Tag the text between the article title and the word "per" or "mus." as <genre>.
  • If the <genre> is followed by "per", tag the following as <instrumentation>. Then, tag the text following "di" as <author>. (See example file deu30001.xml)
  • If the <genre> is followed by "mus.", tag the following text as <author>. Then, tag the texxt following "libr." as <author role="librettist">. (See example file deu300002.xml)
  • Tag information following "rappr." as <rilmPerformance> and child elements <placeName>, <orgName>, or <persName> as available.
  • Tag information following "ediz." with <pubPlace>, <publisher>, or <date> as available.
Articles containing a list of works (example file deu30005):

These are distinguished from articles on a single work by subheadings in bold later in the article.

  • <text> @ type is "topic".
  • Use the first bold text, in small caps as the <title> in the TEI header. Begin the <text> with a <div type="subheading"> and begin the first <p> with a <bibl> element. Tag the bold text as <hi rend="bold"> and <title>. Treat the remainder of the first paragraph the same as for an article on a single work.
  • Start a new <div type="section"> at each of the later bold subheadings in normal case. Use the bold text as <head>, and as the beginning of the following <p><bibl>. Treat the remainder of the paragraph the same as for an article on a single work.
Articles about a fictional or historical person (example files deu30000, deu30003, deu30004):

These can be identified by the word "Personaggio" following the bold article title, OR the absence of "per" or "mus." in the first sentence.

  • <text> @type is "topic".
  • Use the first bold text, in small caps as the <title> in the TEI header. Begin the <text> with a <div type="subheading">. Tag the bold text as <hi rend="bold"> and <persName>. If a variant name appears in parentheses following the bold text, tag it with <forename type="alt"> or <surname type="alt"> as needed.
  • Start a new <div type="section"> at each of the later bold subheadings in normal case. Use the bold text as <head>, and as the beginning of the following <p><bibl>. Treat the remainder of the paragraph the same as for an article on a single work.

NOTE: Some articles may include a list of performances, as in deu30004. Tag as a <list>. Separate items at personal names, which have last name in all CAPS. Tag each as <bibl> and <rilmPerformance> as shown in the example file.

Appendices:

  • Appendice A pages 325–409:
    • Begin the <body> with a <div type="subheading"> containing the header as <head> and the introductory paragraph as <p>.
    • Tag the list as a series of <list> elements; start a new <div type="section"> and <list> at each letter.
    • In each <item>, tag the text up to the parenthesis as <title type="work-short">
    • Tag the italic text as <ref>, and link to the DEUMM article or <div> with the same title.
