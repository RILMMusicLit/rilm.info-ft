Science de la musique (Honegger)
  1. General comments:
    • This publication should be converted from scanned print
    • Articles will have <div type="body"> and <div type="ography>.
    • Bibliographies often have multiple sections.
    • French-style double-angle quotation marks ( « » ) are used.
    • All entries are <text type="topic">
    • In body text, italic type is used to indicate titles, so tag with <title> rather than <hi>.
    • In body text, emphasis is indicated by l e t t e r s p a c i n g.
    • Letter spacing is also used for subsection heads.
    • In bibliographies, titles are in Roman type.
  2. Front matter:
    • Vol. 1: title page; copyright page; "Liste des collaborateurs" (tag as <list>); "Préface"; and "Liste des abréviations" (tag as a <table> with two columns).
      • Note: the Préface is set entirely in Italic type, except for titles, which are in ROMAN type!
    • Vol. 2: only title page; copyright page; and "Liste des abréviations"
  3. Entry heads / titles are in Bold, all caps (no normal case). Tag as <term>. Most are followed by parentheses. In the parentheses may be:
    1. The language of the term in the head;
    2. Translations of the term into other languages;
    3. Etymology
    4. Brief gloss
    Where possible, tag the translations and their languages. Abbreviations and the value for @xml:lang:
    all. = de
    angl. = en
    ital. = it
    esp. = es
    • If there is only one word in the parentheses, capture it in the @xml:lang of the term itself:
      [ONE-STEP (angl.), ...]
      <term xml:lang="en">ONE-STEP</term>
    • Translations are given as "lang., translation; lang., translation". Capture each translated term and its language in a <term> element with @corresp pointing to the main term.
      [PRÉPARATION (angl., preparation; all., Vorbereitung; ital., preparazione; esp., preparaciôn), ...]
      <p><term xml:lang="fr" xml:id="t1">PRÉPARATION<term> (angl., <term xml:lang="en" xml:id="t2" corresp="#t1">preparation</term>; all., <term xml:lang="de" xml:id="t3" corresp="#t1">Vorbereitung</term>; ital., <term xml:lang="it" xml:id="t4" corresp="#t1">preparazione</term>; esp., <term xml:lang="es" xml:id="t5" corresp="#t1">preparación</term>), ...
    • If there is an equal sign ( = ) within the parentheses, it indicates an etymology. In this case, do not try to parse the contents of the parentheses.
  4. Bibliographies are headed "Bibliographie".
    • Individual items are separated by semicolons in the print. Parts of the bibl are separated by commas.
    • Bold face type marks sections; the bold text should be used for the <head> element of each section.
    • Author names in print are typest in small caps, first initial-surname.
    • Titles are in normal case, Roman (not italic!).
    • Article titles are followed by ", in". Periodical titles are followed by vol, date.
    • Omit the dash after head text, the semicolons between items, and the final period.
    [ Bibliographie - F. G. RENDALL, F.W. G., 1858-1945, in Galpin Soc. Journal I, 1948 ; S. GODMAN, F.W. G., Music Maker, in Monthly Musical Record LXXXIV, mars-avril 1954; du meme, Fr.W. G., in Galpin Soc. Journal XII, 1959.]
    <div type="ography"><head>Bibliographie</head>
    <list>
    <item><bibl><author><forename><abbr>F.</abbr></forename> <forename><abbr>G.</abbr></forename> <surname>Rendall</surname></author>, <title>F.W. G., 1858-1945</title>, in <title type="periodical">Galpin Soc. Journal</title> <biblScope unit="vol">I</biblScope>, <date>1948</date></bibl> </item>
    <item><bibl><author><forename><abbr>S.</abbr></forename> <surname>Godman</surname></author>, <title>F.W. G., Music Maker</title>, in <title type="periodical">Monthly Musical Record</title> <biblScope unit="vol">LXXXIV</biblScope>, <date>mars-avril 1954</date></bibl> </item>
    <item><bibl><author>du meme</author>, <title>Fr.W. G</title>., in <title type="periodical">Galpin Soc. Journal</title> <biblScope unit="vol">XII</biblScope>, <date>1959</date></bibl></item>
    </list>
    </div>

  6. See references are in the form [TERM (lang.), voir TARGET.]
    [PRALLTRILLER (all.), voir MORDANT.]
    There can be more than one target. [PRAECENTOR (lat.), voir CANTOR et PRÉCHANTRE.]
  7. "See-also" references are in the form [TERM (lang.) cf également l’art. TARGET.] Make a file for the term, and also as a <keyword> in the target article.
  8. Cross references are indicated by a right-pointing arrow ( → ) preceding the target term.
  9. Back matter comprises tables of illustrations at the back of each volume. Set each as a <table> with three columns.
  10. References to illustrations printed on inserts that are not within the article are made in two steps.
    • A round bullet ( • ) appears next to the first line of the entry, in the page margin.
    • A footnote on the same page, in black sans-serif type, writes "Voir hors-texte entre pages N-N." (see inserts between pages N - N).
    For now, please treat these as footnotes following the first <term> element of the entry. From vol.1 page 9:
    <p xml:id="p1"><term xml:lang="fr" xml:id="t1">Accordéon</term><note place="foot" n="•">Voir hors-texte entre pages 48 - 49.</note> (angl., <term xml:lang="en" xml:id="t2" corresp="#t1">accordion</term>; all., <term xml:lang="de" xml:id="t3" corresp="#t1">Ziehharmonika</term>; ital., <term xml:lang="it" xml:id="t4" corresp="#t1">fisarmonica</term>; esp., <term xml:lang="es" xml:id="t5" corresp="#t1">acordeón</term>), instr. a vent...</p>
