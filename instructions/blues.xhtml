Encyclopedia of the Blues (etb)
  1. This publication should be converted from the provided XML.
  2. From page xxxi to page li, there is a "Thematic list of entries". This will be your guide to the value of @type in the text elemnent:
    Type of entry <text> @type value
    Biographical person
    Cultural topic
    Geographical geo
    Historiography topic
    Instruments instrument
    Music Business topic*
    "American Folk Blues Festival (AFBF)" institution
    "TOBA" institution
    Musical Styles genre
    Musical Techniques topic
    Record labels institution
    Related Art Forms topic
    Song titles work
  3. All articles have the following sections: @heading, @body, and @ography. Many articles will have additional @ography sections, and some will have a new section type: <div type="ref">
  4. Inside the <div type="heading"> should be a <rilmPerson> for person articles, and a <p> for all others. Do not record the formatting of the contents of the heading. <rilmPerson>, the names before the comma should each be in a <surname>, and names after the comma should each be in <forename>. Anything in quotes should be in <addName >. The first example below comes from the source XML. The second is the converted TEI XML.
    <title>Dean, Joe &#x201C;Joe Dean From Bowling Green&#x201D;</title>
    <rilmPerson xml:id="r1"><persName><surname>Dean</surname>, <forename>Joe</forename> <addName>Joe Dean From Bowling</addName><persName>
  5. As seen above, please use @xml:id on all rilmPerson elements from now on.
  6. For "person" articles, the birth and death information should be included in this rilmPerson element:
    <title>Dean, Joe &#x201C;Joe Dean From Bowling Green&#x201D;</title>
    <p>b. 1908; St. Louis, MO</p>
    <p>d. 24 June 1981; St. Louis, MO</p>
    <rilmPerson xml:id="r1"><persName><surname>Dean</surname>, <forename>Joe</forename> <addName>Joe Dean From Bowling</addName><persName><birth><date when="1908" when-custom="1908"> 1908</date>; <placeName>St. Louis, MO</placeName></birth><death><date when="1981-07-24" when-custom="1981">24 June 1981</date><placeName>St. Louis, MO</placeName></death></rilmPerson>
  7. For many person articles, the first paragraph (after the birth and/or death dates) starts with the occupation or a list of occupations. In these cases, tag this passage with another <rilmPerson> element with @corresp pointing to the xml:id of the <rilmPerson> element above it. Tag only those occupations found on the list posted at rilm.info/ft/english-roles.html.
    <div type="head" xml:id="d1"><rilmPerson xml:id="r1"><persName><surname>Green</surname>, <forename>Grant</forename></persName> <birth><date when="1935-06-06" when-custom="1935">6 June 1935</date>; <placeName>St. Louis, MO</placeName></birth><death><date when="1979-01-31" when-custom="1979">31 January 1979</date>; <placeName>New York City, NY</placeName></death></rilmPerson></div> <div type="body" xml:id="d1"> <p><rilmPerson xml:id="r2" corresp="#r1"><occupation>Guitarist</occupation></rilmPerson>. Early influences
    If there are multiple occupations separated by "and" or a comma, they should be tagged separately:
    Cajun <occupation>vocalist</occupation> and <occupation>accordionist</occupation
  8. Some bibliographies, discographies and "selected recordings" lists are inline, where items are separated by semi-colons; some have each item on its own line. In the TEI-XML, you do not need to differentiate between these. All will simply be <list> with <item> children.
  9. See also's: Paragraphs in boldface that begin "See also" should be set as a <list> within a <div type="ref">. Each <item> points to another article in this publication, and is separated by a semi-colon, and each <item> will have a <ref> element with @target pointing to the appropriate article. It will look like this:
    <item><ref target="etb00048">Chenier, Morris <q>Big</q></ref></item>
