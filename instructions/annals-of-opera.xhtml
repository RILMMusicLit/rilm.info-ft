Annals of Opera
  1. General comments:
    • This publication should be converted from scanned print.
    • Each article is dedicated to a single musical work.
    • Articles are arranged chronologically by year.
    • Each article lists performances of the work in paragraph form or list form, or both.
  2. Front matter includes a title page; copyright page; dedication page; Preface; Abbreviations; Introduction; and epithet.
    • Set the Abbreviations list and Principal Theatres list as two-column tables.
  3. Entries are arranged in chronological order by year. The following information is given in the first three lines of type:
    1. Composer surname in small caps, colon, work title in Italic type
    2. Date of premiere in italic type, period, city (and venue) of the premiere
    3. Text author name, period, structure of the work
    Various informations follow, depending on the entry. Treat the first line as a <div type="heading">. Tag the lines as
    1. <rilmPerson> and <title>
    2. <rilmPerformance type="ur">
    3. another <rilmPerson>
  4. Performances may appear in a list or in a paragraph. The example files show both styles of presentation. For items in a list, tag as a <list> with each <item> containing a <rilmPerformance>.
  5. The Back Matter comprises four indexes: (1) Operas (meaning "titles"); 2. Composers; 3. Librettists; 4. General.
    • Tag the Index of Operas as a <list>
    • For the indexes of Composers and Librettists, add the composer and librettist into the TEI header of the appropriate entry as shown in the example files. Add an @corresp attribute to link the name with the form in the <div type="heading">.
    • Treat the General index as per the general instructions.
