Lexikon Progressive Rock

An encyclopedia, in German, of persons, groups, instruments, and terms. To be converted from provided PDF files.

  1. Front matter
    • Omit the half-title (pdf p.1)
    • Title page (pdf p.3)
    • Copyright page (pdf p.4)
    • "Inhalt" (contents, pdf p.7): tag as a two-column table.
    • "Vorwort" (foreword, pdf pp. 11-13): Tag in two <div type="section"> and one <div type="author">
    • Omit the quarter title page (pdf p. 15).
    • Inward-pointing Guillemet- style quotation marks ( » « ) are used. Convert to <q> tags.
  2. Articles will have <div type="body">, and up to four <div type="ography>.
    • Person articles begin with the surname in bold, followed by a comma and the forename(s). The way to distinguish them from other types of article is by the presence of an asterisk * in the first sentence not preceded by a left parenthesis.
      • The general pattern will be [Surname, Forename(s), nationality occupation (occupation, occupation), * Birthplace (Birthplace) Birthdate, † Deathplace]. Most names will not have a death date.

        [Harrison, Gavin, britischer Rockmusiker (Schlagzeug, Perkussion), * Harrow (London) 28. 5. 1963]
        <rilmPerson><persName><surname>Harrison</surname>, <forename>Gavin</forename></persName>, <nationality>britischer</nationality> <occupation>Rockmusiker</occupation> (<occupation>Schlagzeug</occupation>, <occupation>Perkussion</occupation>), <birth><placeName>Harrow</placeName> (<placeName>London</placeName>) <date>28. 5. 1963</date></birth></rilmPerson>
      • Many names will have an alternate form, in forename-surname order, following the word "eigentlich":

        [Niemen, Czesław, eigentlich Czesław Juliusz Wydrzycki, polnischer Rockmusiker (Gesang, Keyboards, Komposition, Text), * Wasiliszki Stare (heute Weißrussland) 16.2. 1939, † Warschau 17.1. 2004;]
        <rilmPerson><persName><surname>Niemen</surname>, <forename>Czesław</forename></persName>, eigentlich <persName type="alt"><forename>Czesław</forename> <forename>Juliusz</forename> <surname>Wydrzycki</surname></persName>, <nationality>polnischer</nationality> <occupation>Rockmusiker</occupation> (<occupation>Gesang</occupation>, <occupation>Keyboards</occupation>, <occupation>Komposition</occupation>, <occupation>Text</occupation>), <birth><placeName>Wasiliszki Stare</placeName> (heute <placeName>Weißrussland</placeName>) <date>16.2. 1939</date></birth>, <death><placeName>Warschau</placeName> <date>17.1. 2004</date></death></rilmPerson>
      • Use the bold text, comma, and following word as the TEI <title>:
        <title>Harrison, Gavin</title> <title>Niemen, Czesław</title>
    • Articles on Groups contain the word "Band" or "Rockband", or "Rockgruppe" in the first sentence. Tag the bold text before the comma as <orgName type="performing"> and use as the TEI <title>:

      [It Bites, britische Progressive-Rock-Band, 1982 in Egremont (Cumbria, England) gegründet.]
      <orgName type="performing">It Bites</orgName>, britische Progressive-Rock-Band, 1982 in Egremont (Cumbria, England) gegründet.
      [Jethro Tull, britische Rockgruppe, 1967 in Luton (Bedfordshire, England) gegründet.]
      <orgName type="performing">Jethro Tull<orgName type="performing">, britische Rockgruppe, 1967 in Luton (Bedfordshire, England) gegründet.
    • Topic articles have neither asterisk nor "Band" or "Rockband" nor "Rockgruppe" in the first sentence. Use the bold text as TEI <title> and tag as <term>.

      NB: Text in the pattern (* 0000) or (* 0000, † 0000) indicates birth- or birth-and-death dates and may appear in any type of article. Include two words before the first parenthesis and the dates in a <rilmPerson> element. Omit the asterisk and/or dagger:

      [Island Records, jamaikanisches Schallplatten-Label, 1959 von Chris Blackwell (* 1937) mit Hilfe von...]
      <term>Island Records</term>, jamaikanisches Schallplatten-Label, 1959 von <rilmPerson><persName><forename>Chris</forename> <surname>Blackwell</surname></persName> <birth>(<date>1937</date>)</birth></rilmPerson> mit Hilfe von...
    • Article body text may be followed by any combination of Literatur, Diskografie, Videography and Weblink. Tag each as a separate list.
      1. Diskografie and Videografie items are separated by squares ( ■ ); The format of each is [Performer: Title (date)] Both the performer and date are optional.

        [Christoph Stiefel: Isorhythms for Solo Piano (2005)]
        <bibl><author><persName><forename>Christoph</forename> <surname>Stiefel</surname></persName></author>: <title>Isorhythms for Solo Piano</title> (<date>1991</date>)</bibl>
        [Vanilla Fudge: The Beat Goes On]
        <bibl><orgName>Vanilla Fudge</orgName>: <title>The Beat Goes On</title></bibl>
        [Quantum Guitar (1998)]
        <bibl><title>Quantum Guitar</title> (<date>1998</date>)</bibl>
      2. Literature items are likewise separated by squares, in the form

        Lastname, Firstname: Title; Pubplace editiondate
      3. Weblink items, if more than one, are separated by squares. They are in the form

        http://url/ (note)
    • See-references and cross-references are both indicated with a single upward-pointing arrow (↑) preceding the target text.
      1. See references are in the form [Referring Term, ↑ Target Term]. Treat these per http://rilm.info/ft/tei_rilm_instructions.html#see-refs.
      2. Cross references in body text are indicated by an upward-pointing arrow before the target term. Tag as <ref> and omit the arrow character.
      3. If there is no match on the word following the arrow, attempt to match a two-words phrase.
    • Back matter comprises a bibliography (Literatur), and an index of corporate names (Firmen, Marken...)
      1. Tag the bibliography as three sections, as shown in the lpr-example4.xml file.
      2. Tag the index as a <list>
