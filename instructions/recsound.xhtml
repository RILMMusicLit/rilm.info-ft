Encyclopedia of Recorded Sound (ers)

  1. This publication should be converted from XML.
  2. All articles have at least two sections: <div type="heading"> and <div type="body">. Some will have a third <div type="ref"> (see below).
  3. The titles of articles appear in two places: (1) above the article itself, and (2) in the Index followed by page references tagged <bold>. The following markers can appear in either place. If the title of an article ends with dates in parentheses, it should have <text type="person">; If the title of an article ends with the word "LABEL" in parentheses, it should have <text type="institution">; If the title of an article ends with "CO.", "LTD.", "CORP." (possible before a parenthetical word), it should have <text type="institution">; If the title of an article ends with the word "BAND" or "music group" in parentheses, it should have <text type="group">; All others should have <text type="generic">.
  4. See references: see above
  5. See also references:
    • within entries: Paragraphs in boldface that begin "See also" should be set as a <list> within a <div type="ref">. Each <item> points to another article in this publication, and is separated by a semi-colon, and each <item> will have a <ref> element with @target pointing to the appropriate article. It will look like this:
      <item><ref target="ers00035">Cassette Deck</ref></item>
    • in the index: no special treatment
  6. Apostrophes: all of the apostrophes in the XML are U+0027, which doesn't match the PDF image. Leave them as they are in the provided XML. Replace only double quotation marks with XML elements--do not replace any single quotes with XML elements.
  7. Italics and quotes: there is one patterns to look for (the title can be in quotes or italics):
    • “[Title]” ([Label] (#)[Number]; [year]; ...)
      Source: “Satan” (Ffrr/London #850990; 1997; #3 U.K.; recorded live in New York)
      TEI-XML: <bibl><title type="work-short">Satan</title> (<publisher>Ffrr/London</publisher> #<idno type="label">850990</idno>; <date>1997</date>; #3 U.K.; recorded live in New York)</bibl>
      Source: <italic>Orbital II</italic> (Ffrr/London #351026; 1993; #28 U.K.)
      TEI-XML: <bibl><title>Orbital II</title> (<publisher>Ffrr</publisher><publisher>London</publisher> #<idno type="label">351026</idno>; <date>1993</date>; #28 U.K.)</bibl>
  8. Bibliography and references: Create one file for the Bibliography and Key to Citations (page 2381-2419) with <text type="matter"> and one <div type="body">. The bibliography will be a list. Here is a crosswalk for marking up that list:

  9. List of Sound Recording Periodicals (pages 2013-2046): Mark this list up as a <list> within the <div type="body">. Each item will then have a <bibl> within an <item>. All italicized text in this list should be marked as <title type="periodical">. The following elements will be used in this list: <title type="periodical">, <date from="" to="">, <pubPlace>, <measure type="frequency">, <bibl>, and <relatedItem> as in the following example:
    <item><bibl><title type="periodical">Studio Sound and Broadcast Engineering.</title> <date from="1992" to="1995">1992&#x2013;1995</date>. <pubPlace>London, England</pubPlace>. <measure type="frequency">Monthly</measure>. Superseded by <relatedItem><bibl><title>Studio Sound.</title></bibl></relatedItem></bibl>
    Entries such as #14 that have a title followed by "see" should link to the title following "see":
    <item n="14"><bibl xml:id="b14"><title>Edison Amberola Monthly</title>. See <ref target="#b15">Edison Phonograph Monthly</ref>.</bibl></item>
    Titles following certain keywords will be tagged within <relatedItem><bibl>. Those keywords are:

    Later titles: Merged into Merged with Superseded by Supersedes Title varies: Variant title(s):
