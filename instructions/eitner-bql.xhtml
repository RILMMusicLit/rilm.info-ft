Biographisch - Bibliographisches
Quellen-Lexikon
  1. General comments:
    • TEI for this publication should be produced from provided text PDFs and scanned print. The PDFs contain corrected text from OCR. The text will differ from the print in a few details:
      1. German-style quotation marks in the print are changed to American-style double quotes in the PDFs.
      2. Long "s" characters (ſ - hex 017F) in the print are replaced with modern lower-case "s".
      3. Some bold face in the print is rendered as Roman in the PDFs.
      However, column breaks and page breaks match the print throughout.
    • Please insert <pb> elements to indicate page breaks in the original.
    • BQL was printed in ten volumes over a period of five years, and different volumes will have different values of <date> in the <imprint> element:
      vols. 1–3 1900
      vols. 4–5 1901
      vols. 6–7 1902
      vols. 8–9 1903
      vol. 10 1904
  2. Front matter includes the title page, Subscribentenliste, Vorwort, Verzeichnis der angezogenen neueren Quellenwerke, Verzeichnis der Bibliotheks-Abkürzungen, Wortabkürzungen mit Ausschluss der allgemein gebräuchlichen, and Vorbemerkung.
    • Set the Subscribentenliste as a <list>. There is no systematic way to distinguish persons from institutions, so simply set each as an <item>. The last word in each item before a parenthesis (if there is one) is a place name:
      <list><item>Paulus Museum in <placeName>Worms</placeName> (Prof. Dr. Weckerling) 1 </item>
      <item>Kirchenchor St. Marien, <placeName>Zwickau</placeName> (Reinh. Vollhardt) 1 </item>
      <item>Dr. Wilh. Bäumker, Pfarrer, <placeName>Rurich</placeName> 1</item></list>
    • The Verzeichnis der angezogenen neueren Quellenwerke is a bibliography. Make this a <text type="matter"> with <div type="body"> for the introductory paragraph ("Diejenigen Titel...") and <div type="ography"> for the list.
      1. Set the first word of each item in <author><surname> tags; the phrase between the first period OR ending parenthesis and second period as <title rend="roman">; and the four-digit number in <date>.
        <item><bibl><author><persName><surname>Pougin</surname></persName>. <title rend="roman">Supplement zu Fetis Biographie universelle</title> 2. Ausg. 2 Bde. Paris <date>1878</date>. <date>1880</date>.</bibl></item>

        <item><bibl><author><persName><surname>Prost</surname> (B.)</persName></author> <title rend="roman">Liste des artistes mentionnes dans les etats de la maison du roi et des maisons des Princes, du 13. siecle ä Tan 1500</title>. In Archives historiques. Paris T. 1. <date>1889</date>. S. 431 ff.</bibl></item>

        <item><bibl><author><persName><surname>Prüfer</surname> (A.) </persName></author> <title rend="roman">Untersuchungen über den außerkirchlichen Kunstgesang in den evangelichen Schulen des 16. Jhs. Lpz. 1890 Fock. Mit Musikbeilagen.</bibl></item>
      2. For the entries "A. d. B.", "Biographien", "Denkmäler", "Jahrbuch", "Lpz. Ztg.", "Magazin", "N. Z. f. M.", "Niederrheinische", "Publikation", "Tijdschrift", and "Viertelj.", omit the <author><persName><surname> and <title> tagging.
        <item><bibl>Publikation älterer praktischer und theoretischer Musikwerke vorzugsweise des 15. u. 16. Jhs. Herausgeg. von der Gesellschaft f. Musikforschung. 1. Jahrg. <date>1873</date> bis 27. Jahrg. <date>1899</date>. Lpz. Br. & H.</bibl></item>
    • Set the Verzeichnis der Bibliotheks-Abkürzungen and the Wortabkürzungen as simple <list> elements.
  3. Three kinds of entries will be found:
    • See-references
    • Person entries with article text
    • Entries without article text, either Person or Topic
  4. See references in this publication should be treated as described in the general instruction page. They are indicated in one of six ways in the source:
    • with the word "ist" (is): [A. A. ist Alexander Agricola] Note that the target entry will be alphabetized by the bold name, i.e. "Agricola, Alexander".
    • with the word "gleich" (equals): [A. B. gleich Anton Brumel]
    • With the word "siehe" (see): [Cociolini, siehe Conciolini.]
    • With the abbreviation "s.": [Colbanus, Simon, s. Kolbanus.]
    • With an equal sign: [F. D. = Fréderic Desessart.] Note that the target entry will be alphabetized by the italicized name; "Desessart, Frederic"
    • With the word "mutmasslich" (perhaps, possibly)
    Where there is more than one target given with "und" or "u.", place a <keyword> element in each of the target articles:
    [Fockerod, siehe Fokkerodt u. Vockerodt.]
  5. Each entry will have <div type="body"> and one or both of <div type="ography"> and <div type="works">. There will be no <div type="heading">.
  6. Person entries begin with the personal name given in surname, forename order, typeset in Bold. The text of the article, if there is any, continues on the same line in Roman type following a comma or period:
    [Holan, Wenzeslaus Karl, geb. gegen 1645 zu Rowno in Böhmen, war Organist an der Peter-Paul- Kirche in Prag.]
    Start the first <p> of the body and tag from the beginning up to the first period or semicolon as a <rilmPerson>.
    • Birth and death informations may appear in the first sentence of the body and are indicated as follows:
      • Birth dates are preceded by "geboren" or "geb." ; and the place is preceded by "zu" or "in".
      • Death dates are be preceded by "gest." or by a dagger (†) or by "starb" or by "st.".
      Because the original text is inconsistent in the way it identifies birth and death dates, please tag them as <birth rend="noMarker"> and <death rend="noMarker"> and do not omit any characters, including dagger characters.
    • Occupations are also often given in the first sentence. If a word in this list of occupations is found between the beginning of the entry and the first period or semicolon, please tag it <occupation>.
      <div type="body"><p><rilmPerson><persName><surname>Holan</surname>, <forename>Wenzeslaus</forename> <forename>Karl</forename></persName>, <birth rend="noMarker">geb. gegen <date when="1645" when-custom="1645">1645</date> zu <placeName>Rowno</placeName> in <placeName>Böhmen</placeName></birth>, war <occupation>Organist</occupation> an der Peter-Paul- Kirche in Prag.</rilmPerson></p></div>
    • When names include variant forms in in parentheses, variants before the comma should be assumed to be surnames and variants after the comma should be assumed to be forenames. Each variant surname and/or forename should be tagged in its own <persName> element. Words in the parentheses not beginning with a capital letter should not be tagged.

      [Hoioul (Hoyoul, Hoyul), Johann Ludwig,]
      <persName><surname>Hoioul</surname></persName> (<persName><surname>Hoyoul</surname></persName>, <persName><surname>Hoyul</surname></persName>), <persName><forename>Cornelis</forename></persName>,
      [Heekel, Wolf (Wolfgang),]
      <persName><surname>Heekel</surname>, <forename>Wolf</forename></persName> (<persName><forename>Wolfgang</forename></persName>),
    • Titles ("Dr.", "Rev.", "Fra.", "Don", "D.", "Pat.", "Pater", "Abbé", "l'Abbé") are set in non-bold type between surname and forename with no parentheses and should be enclosed in <roleName> elements.

      [Riddell, Captain Robert]
      <persName><surname>Riddell</surname>, <roleName>Captain</roleName> <forename>Robert</forename></persName>
    • Where only the first letter (or letters) of the forename is known, the name is given with the letter(s) and elipsis (three periods): [Arnulfus, G . . . ,] In such cases, include the elipsis within the <forename> tags:
      <persName><surname>Arnulfus</surname>, <forename>G. . . </forename></persName>,
    • Where only the surname is known, an elipsis is used in place of the forename: [Arthon, . . .]. Again, tag the elipsis as <forename>:
      <persName><surname>Arthon</surname>, <forename>. . .</forename></persName>
  7. Lists following articles:
    • If there is no head at the top of the list following the body of an article, tag it as a <div type="ography"> containing <item> and <bibl> elements. The formatting is often too irregular to determine titles, etc., reliably, so do not divide the <bibl> elements.
    • Some lists have heads in italic type.
      1. If the list has an italic head containing "Bibliographie" or "Biographie", mark up the list following it as a <div type="ography"> with the title in a <head> element. Many entries in this type of list begin with the author's name followed by a colon (:):

        [Beyle, Henry: Vies de H., de Mozart . . . Paris 1854 Lévy frères. 12º. 341 S. [Bologna.]

        Tag the author's name where there is a colon, and tag the date:
        <item><bibl><author><surname>Beyle</surname>, <forename>Henry</forename></author>: Vies de H., de Mozart . . . Paris <date>1854</date> Lévy frères. 12º. 341 S. [Bologna.</bibl></item>
      2. Lists with a centered italic head containing "Komposition", "werke", "musik", or "instrumental" should be tagged <div type="works">. Tag each indented entry with <item> and <bibl>. Where such a head begins with a number, it marks a "section" within a longer "works" list. (Please see the file bql-sample-3.xml)
  8. Entries without article text:
    • Some entries have a punctuation after the head, then a line break. The smaller-type "ography" begins immediately:

      [Kolbe, . . .
      die B. B. besitzt einen französischen
      Druck von „4 Fughe‟ ohne Titelbl. in
      fol., dessen Druckprivileg von König Louis
      am 11. Mai 1730 ausgefertigt ist.]
      <div type="body"><p><rilmPerson>
      <persName><surname>Kolbe</surname>, <forename>. . .</forename></persName></rilmPerson></p></div>
      <div type="ography">
      <list><item><bibl>die B. B. besitzt einen französischen Druck von 4 Fughe ohne Titelbl. in fol., dessen Druckprivileg von König Louis am 11. Mai 1730 ausgefertigt ist.</bibl></item></list></div>
    • Entries that have no comma before the first line break are <text type="topic">. Tag the text in Bold face type as <seg function="topic> and <term>.

      [Fondamento
      par l'accompagnamento. Ms. [Musikfr. Wien.]
      <div type="body"><p><seg type="topic"><term>Fondamento</term></seg></p></div>
      <div type="ography"><list><item><bibl>par l'accompagnamento. Ms. [Musikfr. Wien.</bibl></item></list></div>
      [Eastcheap.
      Practical discourses of singing in the worship of God, preached at Eastcheap by several Ministers. London 1708, 8°. [Glasgow, br. Mus.
      <div type="body"><p><seg type="topic"><term>Eastcheap</term></seg>.</p></div>
      <div type="ography"><list><item><bibl>Practical discourses of singing in the worship of God, preached at Eastcheap by several Ministers. London 1708, 8°. [Glasgow, br. Mus.</bibl></item></list></div>
  9. A handful of entries imply family relationships and are typeset with a dash at the beginning of the entries after the first:

    [Berkenhof, Franz Norbert, aus Leyden, ist am 12. Nov. 1759 an der Universität zu Leyden als Musicus eingeschrieben (Bouwst. 3, 8).
    — Hermann Gabriel, aus Leyden, ebendort am 28. Mai 1768 als Musicus eingeschrieben (ib, 3, 9).
    — Theodor Albert, aus Leyden, ebendort am 7. März 1767 u. 24. Okt. 1789 als Musicus eingezeichnet. Er war geb. um 1746, gest. um 1814 u. Violist u. Stadt-Musikmeister zu Leyden (Bouwst. 3, 9 u. 11).]

    Treat each as a separate entry and replicate the family name ("Berkenhof, Hermann Gabriel" and "Berkenhof, Theodor Albert") where it has been replaced with a dash.
  10. Verbesserungen und Nachträge sections at the ends of volumes should be set as <list>.
