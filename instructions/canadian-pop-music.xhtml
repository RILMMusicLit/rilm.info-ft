Canadian Pop Music Encyclopedia

An encyclopedia, in English, of persons and groups. To be converted from two provided MS Word files.

  1. Front matter:
    • Title page, with image
    • Copyright page
    • OMIT the Contents list
    • Acknowledgements (including the dedication at the top of p.7): begin a new <div> at Grace Notes; ends p.9
    • How to Use and Understand this Encyclopedia: begin a new <div> at Definitions; tag the three-column section at the bottom of p.11 as a <list>; begin a new <div> at Submissions, and continue through p.13.
    • Introduction (pp.14-15)
    • NOTE: Volume 2 front matter is identical with Volume 1 front matter, so it can be omitted.
  2. Articleson Groups:
    • Articles on Groups have either (a) a heading in all caps ("ADVENTURES IN PARADISE") OR (b) a heading ending with ", The" ("ANTIQUATORS, The" ). Use the string as //titleStmt/title in the TEI header, and in <div type="heading"> in the TEI body, as shown in the example files.
    • Most group articles have a list of group members after the article heading. The members are delimited by " / ". Tag each item as a <rilmPerson>; inside, tag names and occupation:

      [Dwayne Ford (keyboards, flute, guitar, vocals) / Hugh Brockie (lead and rhythm guitar, banjo) / Terry Danko (bass; later switched to guitar) / Jim Atkinson (vocals, guitar) / Brian Hilton (drums) / Penti 'Whitey' Glan (percussion) / Chris Vickery (bass; replaced Danko) / Marty Coudrey (drums; replaced Mal Turner) ]
      <p><rilmPerson><persName><forename>Dwayne</forename> <surname>Ford</surname></persName> (<occupation>keyboards</occupation>, <occupation>flute</occupation>, <occupation>guitar</occupation>, <occupation>vocals</occupation>)</rilmPerson> / <rilmPerson><persName><forename>Hugh</forename> <surname>Brockie</surname></persName> (<occupation>lead and rhythm guitar</occupation>, <occupation>banjo</occupation>)</rilmPerson> / <rilmPerson><persName><forename>Terry</forename> <surname>Danko</surname></persName> (<occupation>bass</occupation>; later switched to guitar)</rilmPerson> / <rilmPerson><persName><forename>Jim</forename> <surname>Atkinson</surname></persName> (<occupation>vocals</occupation>, <occupation>guitar</occupation>)</rilmPerson> / <rilmPerson><persName><forename>Penti</forename> '<addName>Whitey</addName>' <surname>Glan</surname></persName> (<occupation>percussion</occupation>)</rilmPerson> / <rilmPerson><persName><forename>Chris</forename> <surname>Vickery</surname></persName> (<occupation>bass</occupation>; replaced Danko)</rilmPerson> / <rilmPerson><persName><forename>Marty</forename> <surname>Coudrey</surname></persName> (<occupation>drums</occupation>; replaced Mal Turner)</rilmPerson> </p>
      (Note: don't tag explanations following the semicolon.)
    • The body text of all articles consists of a single paragraph. Tag any phrase within quotation marks (single or double, curly or straight) as <title>.
    • Some articles have a list of contributors, in bold italic, at the end of the body text. These lists begin with "with notes from". Begin a new <p> and tag as italic:
      [The group disbanded in late 1986/early 1987. with notes from Paul Williamson.]
      ...The group disbanded in late 1986/early 1987.</p>
      <p><hi rend="italic">with notes from Paul Williamson.</hi></p>
    • Some articles have see-also references, which are in bold italic, set off with brackets, and beginning with the text "[also see". Begin a new <p>, tag as bold, and tag the ref targets:
      [...Saturday, October 16, 2004. [also see DOUG & THE SLUGS] ]
      ...Saturday, October 16, 2004.</p>
      <p><hi rend="bold">[also see <ref target="cpm00000">DOUG & THE SLUGS</ref>]</hi></p>
    • Discographies consist of maximum three sections: Singles, Albums, and Compilation Tracks. Set each as a separate <div>. Within each of those three sections, there may be subsections headed with "as..." or "with...". These should be tagged as nested <div>s. SEE the file cmp_example2.xml for the structure. Tag <date>, <title>, <publisher>, and <idno> as shown in the example files.
  3. Person articles have a heading in all caps followed by a comma, and normal-capped text that is NOT "The"; this is often followed by Birth/Death data:
    BERGMANN, Art [Born: February 8, 1953 in Vancouver, British Columbia].
    The are otherwise identical in structure and format to group articles. SEE the file cmp_example1.xml.
  4. Treat see-references (such as EAST AFRICAN AFFAIR [see INFERNO 5 PLUS 1] as per http://rilm.info/ft/tei_rilm_instructions.html#see-refs.
