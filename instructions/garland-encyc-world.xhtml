Garland Encyclopedia of World Music, The
  1. General observations.
    • This publication should be converted from the provided PDF files.
    • Each line in the table of contents of each volume should correspond with a TEI XML file.
    • Double [“ ”] quotation marks are used for quotes, but single ones [‘ ’] are used for translations of non-English terms:
      [...musique Zaïroise moderne ‘modern Zairean music’...]
      Use <q> for double quotes, <q rend="single"> for the latter type.
    • Parts of this publication were produced prior to the common adoption of Unicode. Diacritic markings may appear as separate characters in the PDF texts ("tu¨rku¨", for example). These should be replaced with Unicode characters ("türkü").
  2. Front matter items are indicated in the table of contents before the text "Part 1".
  3. Lists of topics at the beginnings of articles, in bold sans-serif type, duplicate section headings in the article, and may be omitted. (See example files.)
  4. Sections within an article are at three levels.
    • Higher-level sections are indicated by all-capital, bold, sans-serif headings with a large outdent.
    • Mid-level sections are indicated by headings in normal-case, bold, sans-serif type aligned with the left margin.
    • Lowest-level sections are indicated by headings in normal-case, bold, sans-serif slanted type.
    Lower-level sections should be nested within higher ones [from vol. 1, p. 8]:
    <div type="section"><heading>CONCEPTS OF PERFORMANCE</heading>
    <p>Some generalizations can be drawn about performance in Africa, emphasizing the perspectives of the performers and their ideas about creating that performance. However, we must bear in mind that great variation exists, even about fundamental ideas.</p>
    <div type="section"><heading>Performers</heading>
    <p>To some extent, most people in African communities are expected to perform music and dance at a basic level...</p>
    </div>
    </div>
  5. Pull quotes in large roman type at the tops of pages (vol. 1, p.8, p.10, for example) should be tagged as a <div type="textBox"> containing a <p>. Position them before the <div> within which they appear on the page.
  6. For glossaries of terms that appear at the heads of pages (vol. 1, p.14, for example), create a <list> element before the <p> within with they appear on the page. Tag the bold terms or names as <term> or <persName>, with @rend="bold".
  7. References to sound recording tracks (A small CD icon with "TRACK x"; see vol. 1, p. 366, for example) can be represented by a <ref> element with @target "trackx.mp3".
  8. Bibliographies are printed at the end of most articles.
    • The first author names are given in surname-forename order; any subsequent authors are given in forename-surname order:
      [Ssalongo, Christopher Kizza, and Peter Cooke, ]
      <author><surname>Ssalongo</surname>, <forename>Christopher</forename> <forename>Kizza</forename></author>, and <author><forename>Peter</forename> <surname>Cooke</surname></author>,
    • The <date> element will always follow immediately after the author or authors.
    • Set quoted titles in <title type="article"> and italic titles in <title> without attribute.

Please refer also to the provided xml samples.
