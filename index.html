RILM Encoding Instructions and Samples

File Formats

Full Text

Material that requires scanning should be be converted into uncompressed TIFF files. Please use the following document for guidelines: ImagingStandardsforDigitization.pdf

For journals:

TIFF files will be converted in TEI-XML and PDF format.

PDFs should conform to PDF 1.6 with searchable text and tagged page content.

Checklist for licensed/processed content

RILM controls content received for the following faults. Items failing any of these tests are unacceptable.

✓ Complete item, cover to cover.
✓ No editing errors (missing fonts, orphaned headings, image placeholders, etc.).
✓ Solid black text on a white background: too dark or too light will fail.
✓ Color or greyscale images as appropriate (see Imaging Standards at http://rilm.info/ft/ImagingStandardsforDigitization.pdf).
✓ One full page per image, no spreads. Front and back cover as separate images, without spine. Each page cropped to page boundaries: no crop marks, color bars, or file information in bleed/slug area. Consistent layout throughout item.
✓ Text, footnotes, music examples, tables, charts, etc. all legible.
✓ Scanned material is laid flat and within 2° of straight (deskewed) on scanner: straight lines of text, no or minimal gutter shadow.
✓ No MS marks (library stamps OK).
✓ No or minimal print show through, speckles, blotches, or discoloration.
✓ OCR or PDF text layer, if present, at 99.6% accuracy or above, correct language(s)/character set(s) selected.

For encyclopedias:

TIFF files will be converted into TEI-XML format; roughly 65% of the encyclopedia pages will also require conversion into METS/ALTO plus jpeg images generated at 300 ppi. A full specification will be provided at a later date.

* Depending on the cost estimates, we may elect to have journals converted only into PDF, and to have selected encyclopedias converted only into METS-ALTO.

Headers and References

Headers and references will be recorded in TEI files. Samples and instructions will be provided.
