Sachlexikon Rockmusik

A two-volume encyclopedia, in German, of terms and institutions related to rock and popular musik. To be converted from provided PDF files.

  1. Front matter
    • Omit the half-title (pdf p.1)
    • Title page (pdf p.3)
    • Copyright page (pdf p.4)
    • "Inhalt" (contents, pdf p.7): tag as a two-column table.
    • "Vorwort" (foreword, pdf pp. 11-13): Tag in four <div type="section"> and one <div type="author">
    • Omit the quarter title page (pdf p. 15).
    • Inward-pointing Guillemet- style quotation marks ( » « ) are used. Convert to <q> tags.
  2. Articles will have <div type="body">, and from 0 to three <div type="ography>.
    • Article body texts begin with a heading in bold followed by a comma.
      1. Use the bold text as the TEI <title> and as the beginning of the div type="body".
      2. Tag the beginning bold text as <term>, and any subsequent bold text as <term> with @corresp to the first.
      3. Text in the pattern (* 0000) or (* 0000, † 0000) indicates birth- or birth-and-death dates. Include two words before the first parenthesis and the dates in a <rilmPerson> element. Omit the asterisk and dagger:

        [...Komponisten und Arrangeur Ronald Binge (* 1910, † 1979)...]
        <p>...Komponisten und Arrangeur <rilmPerson><persName><forename>Ronald</forename> <surname>Binge</surname></persName> (<birth><date when="1910" when-custom="1910">1910</date></birth>, <death><date when="1979" when-custom="1979">1979</date></death>)</rilmPerson>...</p>
    • Article body text may be followed by any combination of Literatur, Diskografie, Videography and Weblink. Tag each as a separate list.
      1. Diskografie and Videografie items are separated by squares ( ■ ); The format of each is

        Performer: Title (date; note)

        The note following the year is optional.
      2. Literature items are likewise separated by squares, in the form

        Lastname, Firstname: Title; Pubplace editiondate
      3. Weblink items, if more than one, are separated by squares. They are in the form

        http://url/ (note)
  3. See-references and cross-references are both indicated with a single upward-pointing arrow (↑) preceding the target text.
    • See references are in the form [Referring Term, ↑ Target Term]. Treat these per http://rilm.info/ft/tei_rilm_instructions.html#see-refs.
    • Cross references in body text are indicated by an upward-pointing arrow before the target term. Tag as <ref> and omit the arrow character.
    • If there is no match on the word following the arrow, attempt to match a two words phrase.
  4. Back matter comprises a bibliography (Literatur), and an index of corporate names (Firmen, Marken...)
    1. Tag the bibliography as three sections, as shown in the srm-example4.xml file.
    2. Tag the index as a <list>

Please refer also to sample files.
