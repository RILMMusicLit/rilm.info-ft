Dictionnaire des œuvres de l'art vocal (Honegger)
  1. General comments:
    • This publication should be converted from scanned print
    • Articles will have <div type="body"> and <div type="ography>.
    • Bibliographies will have up to three sections.
    • French-style double-angle quotation marks ( « » ) are used.
    • All entries are <text type="work">
    • In body text, italic type is used to indicate titles, so tag with <title> rather than <hi>.
    • In bibliographies, titles are in Roman type.
  2. Front matter:
    • Vol. 1: title page; copyright page; "Liste des collaborateurs" (tag as <list>); "Introduction"; "Avant-propos"; and "Liste des abréviations" (tag as a <table> with two columns).
      • Note: the Introduction is set entirely in Italic type, except for titles, which are in ROMAN type!
    • Vol. 2: title page; copyright page; "Liste des collaborateurs"; "Note au lecteur"; and "Liste des abréviations"
    • Vol. 3: title page; copyright page only. We do not need to encode the remaining front matter as it is identical with that in vol. 2.
  3. Entry heads / titles are in Bold, small caps. Convert to normal case. Tag as <title>.
  4. The genre of the work follows directly or shortly after the title. Tag as <term type="genre">. See the list below for identification.
  5. References to notation examples that are not printed within the article are made via white capital letters in a black box. Insert the <figure> element following the <p> within which it is referenced.
  6. Bibliographies are headed "Bibliographie".
    • Individual items are separated by semicolons in the print. Parts of the bibl are separated by commas.
    • Author names in print are typest in small caps, first initial-surname.
    • Titles are in normal case, Roman (not italic!).
    • Article titles are followed by ", in". Periodical titles are followed by vol, date.
    • Omit the dash after head text, the semicolons between items, and the final period.
  7. See references are in the form [TITLE, de Named Composer, voir TARGET.]:
    [SECOND LIVRE DES MESLANGES, de Claude Le Jeune, voir LIVRE DE MÉLANGES.]
    If there is more than one target, each target is set in quotes:
    [(IL) SECONDO LIBRO DE MADRIGALI A CINQUE VOCI, de Claudio Monteverdi, voir les articles « ECCO MORMORAR L'ONDE » et « NONSI LEVAV' ANCOR L'ALBA NOVELLA ».]
  8. Cross references are indicated by a right-pointing arrow ( → ) preceding the target term.
  9. Back matter for Vols. 1 and 2 comprises tables of illustrations at the back of each volume. Set each as a <table> with two columns.
  10. Back matter for Vol. 3 is extensive:
    • Glossaire (glossary): Treat each as a separate entry with one <div type="body">; treat see references as usual.
    • Index des compositeurs (index of composers): Each name has a list of entries following. Add the name to each of the listed entries in a <keywords> element in the <profileDesc> of the entry's TEI header. Parse as <rilmPerson>:
      <textClass> <keywords> <term> <rilmPerson> <persName><surname>Sauguet</surname>, <forename>Henri</forename></persName> (<birth><date when="1901" when-custom="1901">1901</date></birth>-<death><date when="1989" when-custom="1989">1989</date></death>) </rilmPerson> </term> </keywords> </textClass>
    • Chronologie des compositeurs: Tag as a <list>; parse names as <rilmPerson>. Do not tag <surname> or <forename> for names without a comma:
      <item><rilmPerson><persName>Théodulphe</persName> (<death><date when="0821" when-custom="0821">821</date></death>)</rilmPerson></item>
      <item><rilmPerson><persName><surname>Vitry</surname>, <forename>Philippe</forename> <nameLink>de</nameLink></persName> (<birth><date when="1291" when-custom="1291">1291</date></birth>-<death><date when="1361" when-custom="1361">1361</date></death>)</rilmPerson></item>
    • Index des poètes, librettistes et écrivains: Treat exactly like the Index des compositeurs.
    • Table des illustrations: set as a <table> with two columns.
