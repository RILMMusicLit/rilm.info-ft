Großes Sängerlexikon, 4th ed. (2003)
  1. General comments:
    • This publication should be converted from the provided rich PDF.
    • This publication has only "person" articles.
    • Articles have only <div type="body"> and possibly up to 2 <div type="ography">.
    • German-style quotation marks (double angle: « and ») are used throughout to indicate titles. OMIT the quotation marks and tag these <title rend="quoted">.
    • Italic type is used to indicate personal names unless used in conjunction with quotation marks (« and »).
  2. Front matter includes the Title page, copyright page, contents, "Vorwort" (Foreword), "Die Autoren" (Authors), and "Abkürzungen von Schallplatten-Marken" (Abbreviations of Record Labels).
    • Set the Abkürzungen list as a <table>.
  3. Entries give the personal name given in surname, forename order. The surname is set in in bold followed by a comma, with surnames in normal-case bold. Next, the occupation(s) is (are) given, followed by another comma. Finally birth and date information is given. Omit the * and † characters. Tag up to the first period or semicolon in a <rilmPerson> element.
    • Pavloska, Irene, Mezzosopran/Sopran, * 1890 in Kanada als Tochter polnischer Eltern, † 12. 2. 1962 Chicago.
    <div type="body">
    <p><rilmPerson><persName><surname>Pavloska</surname>, <forename>Irene</forename></persName>, <occupation>Mezzosopran/Sopran</occupation>, <birth><date when="1890" when-custom="1890">1890</date> in <placeName>Kanada</placeName> als Tochter polnischer Eltern</birth>, <death><date when="1962-12-02" when-custom="1962">12. 2. 1962</date> <placeName>Chicago</placeName></death></rilmPerson>.
    ...</p></div>
    If a period or semicolon is encountered before a * or † character, it means that birth and death information is not given:
    • Payer, Christian, Tenor; er war Mitglied der Kaiserlichen Hofkapelle Wien.
    <div type="body"><p><rilmPerson><persName><surname>Payer</surname>, <forename>Christian</forename>, <occupation>Tenor</occupation></persName></rilmPerson>; er war Mitglied der Kaiserlichen Hofkapelle Wien. ...</p></div>
  4. Bibliographies, if present are indicated by the word "Lit" followed by a colon.
    • Author, if given, comes first in forename-surname order, followed by a colon. [E. Solmière:].
    • Books titles are in angle quotes and are followed by (place, date) in parentheses: [E. Solmière: «Rose Caron» (Paris, 1896).]
      <bibl><author><forename><abbr>E.</abbr></forename> <surname>Solmière</surname></author>: <title rend="quoted">Rose Caron</title> (<pubPlace>Paris</pubPlace>, <date>1896</date>).</bibl>
    • Article titles are not quoted, and are followed by the source in parentheses, source title in quotes: [R. Celletti & R. Vegeto: Lina Cavalieri (in «Le grandi Voci», Rom 1964).
      <bibl><author><forename><abbr>R.</abbr></forename> <surname>Celletti</surname></author> & <author><forename><abbr>R.</abbr></forename> <surname>Vegeto</surname></author>: <title type="article">Lina Cavalieri</title> (in <title rend="quoted">Le grandi Voci</title>, <pubPlace>Rom</pubPlace> <date>1964</date>).</bibl>
    • Dissertations titles are not quoted, and have "Dissertation" in the parenthesis with school and date: [P. Lewy Gidwitz: Vocal Profiles of Four Mozart Sopranos (Dissertation, University of California, Berkeley, 1991).]
      <bibl><author><forename><abbr>P.</abbr></forename> <surname>Lewy</surname> <surname>Gidwitz</surname></author>: <title rend="roman">Vocal Profiles of Four Mozart Sopranos</title> (Dissertation, <orgName type="uni">University of California</orgName>, <pubPlace>Berkeley</pubPlace>, <date>1991</date>).</bibl>
    • NOTE: if the entry does not include a colon, the author is not identified: [Susan Chilcott (in «Opera», 2002)].
      <bibl><title rend="roman">Susan Chilcott</title> (in <title rend="quoted">Opera</title>, <date>2002</date>)
  5. The beginning of a discography, if present, is signaled by the word "Schallplatten" followed by a colon.
    • Each item generally give the record label name first (publisher), followed by additional information in parentheses. Years are sometimes given.
    • Titles are always in double-angle quotation marks. Again, omit the quotation marks.

      [Schallplatten: Philips (Andrej in «Khovantchina»), Decca («Fürst Igor» von Borodin; Dimitrij im «Boris Godunow», auch als Video).]
      <div type="ography"><head>Schallplatten:</head>
      <list>
      <item><bibl><publisher>Philips</publisher> (Andrej in <title rend="quoted">Khovantchina</title>),</bibl></item>
      <item><bibl><publisher>Decca</publisher> (<title rend="quoted">Fürst Igor</title> von Borodin; Dimitrij im <title rend="quoted">Boris Godunow</title>, auch als Video)</bibl></item>
      </list></div>.
    • Some "Schallplatten" sections contain only prose, and there will be no way to parse individual items. Tag whatever can be confidently identified:

      [Schallplatten: Von ihrer technisch hervorragend geführten Koloraturstimme existieren Edison-Zylinder sowie Victor-Platten (1907-08), darunter als bekannteste Aufnahme das «Rigoletto»-Quartett mit Enrico Caruso, Louise Homer und Antonio Scotti.]
      <div type="ography"><head>Schallplatten:</head>
      <list>
      <item><bibl>Von ihrer technisch hervorragend geführten Koloraturstimme existieren Edison-Zylinder sowie Victor-Platten (<date>1907-08</date>), darunter als bekannteste Aufnahme das <title rend="quoted">Rigoletto</title>-Quartett mit Enrico Caruso, Louise Homer und Antonio Scotti.</bibl></item>
      </list></div>.
  6. See references are always in the form "Surname, Forename, s. unter Surname2, Forename2."
  7. Back matter is lengthy.
    • OPERN UND OPERETTEN UND DEREN URAUFFÜHRUNGEN: set the "OPERN" and "OPERETTA" sections in separate files. The items are formatted as: Title in italic type, "von", author, date, place, "mit", and performers separated by slashes:

      [Les Abencérages ou L’Étendard de Grenade von Luigi Cherubini, 6. 4. 1813 Grand Opéra Paris (in Anwesenheit von Kaiser Napoleon I.) mit Alexandrine-Caroline Branchu / Louis Nourrit / Henri-Étienne Dérivis /Jacques-Émile Lavigne /Joséphine Armand / Alexandre /Jean-Honoré Bertin.]
      <item><bibl><title>Les Abencérages ou L’Étendard de Grenade</title> von <author>Luigi Cherubini</author>, <rilmPerformance type="ur"><date when="1813-06-04" when-custom="1813">6. 4. 1813</date> Grand Opéra Paris (in Anwesenheit von Kaiser Napoleon I.) mit <rilmPerson><persName>Alexandrine-Caroline Branchu</persName></rilmPerson> / <rilmPerson><persName>Louis Nourrit</persName></rilmPerson> / <rilmPerson><persName>Henri-Étienne Dérivis</persName></rilmPerson> /<rilmPerson><persName>Jacques-Émile Lavigne</persName></rilmPerson> /<rilmPerson><persName>Joséphine Armand</persName></rilmPerson> / <rilmPerson><persName>Alexandre</persName></rilmPerson> /<rilmPerson><persName>Jean-Honoré Bertin</persName></rilmPerson>.</rilmPerformance></bibl>
    • Tag the introductory paragraphs of the BIBLIOGRAPHIE section as <div type="body">, and the remainder as <div type="ography">. Begin a new <div> with each of the two heads: "A )Werke aus den «Biographischen Archiven» des Saur-Verlags", and "Weitere Quellen:".
      • If an item begins with an author, it is in surname, forename order followed by a colon [Alcari, Cesare:]
      • If there is no colon in the entry either (1) there is no author, or (2) there are editors, after "Hrsg. v." [.... Hrsg. v. Richard Brutigam...] in forename-surname order.
      • Parentheses contain Place and Date, and sometime edition or volume number(s) as [(Tomes 1-27, Bruxelles 1866-1938)] or [(2nd edition London 1991)]. Tag the place and date. Place will always immediate precede the date.
