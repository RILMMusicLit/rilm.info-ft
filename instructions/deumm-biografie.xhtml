Dizionario Enciclopedico Universale della Musica e dei Musicisti [DEUMM] part 1 - Biografie

[*Last Revised 2022/03/01* Revisions are indicated by "*NEW".]

The first stage of RILMs conversion of DEUMM will be the 10-volume Biografie. You will produce a PDF for each of the volumes (scanned at 300 dpi, grayscale, and OCR text in Italian), plus article-level TEI files.

1. General principles:
  • Each volume of the Biografie has a different year and ISBN, given on the copyright page of each. The correct values should be included in the TEI header of each article.
  • xml:id's: DEUMM online will be a continually updated resource, so each <div>, <p>, and <bibl> element must have a unique identifier: an xml:id element containing "id-" followed by a UUID. For example: <p xml:id="id-f11a99bb-5a84-12df-f1b2-b9374b7da8ab">. The following six elements require an @xml:id :
    – <bibl>, <div>, <docAuthor>, <figure>, <p>, <rilmPerson>, <rilmPerformance>
  • The Biografie volumes include articles on persons, families, and corporations.
  • There are short, medium, and long articles.
    • Short articles contain
      • one paragraph of body text, often, but not always, followed by
      • a worklist and/or ~ographies.
    • Medium articles contain
      • one paragraph of body text in normal-sized type, followed by
      • one or more paragraphs in slightly smaller type, then
      • a worklist or ~ographies.
    • Long articles contain
      • multiple sections of body text, with headers in small caps, followed by
      • one or more tabular worklists and
      • one or more ~ographies.
  • Abbreviation of names within articles: In medium and long articles, every mention of the person the article is about is abbreviated to the first letter of the surname after the first paragraph. Such abbreviations should be expanded to the full surname. For example, in the article "Schönbach, Dieter", the second paragraph begins:
    [La produz. di S. prende le mosse,… ]
    The "S." should be expanded to "Schönbach".
  • General abbreviations (listed in front matter): Each volume has a list of Abbreviations ("Abbreviazione") in the front matter. Where these abbreviations are used in the articles, they should be expanded to the full word or phrase. For the example above, the abbreviation "produz." is listed in the Abbreviazione as "produzione". For the example given above, the full rendition should be:
    <p>La produzione di Schönbach prende le mosse,...
  • *NEW 3/1/2022* Identification of Named individuals with IDs from RILMs database of persons: RILM will provide a list of personal names and dates along with integer IDs, in .csv or other similar format. The <rilmPerson> tag applied to the name of the person the article is about should have an @xml:id attribute and a @key attribute that contains the ID. For example, "Schönbach, Dieter" appears in the list with ID "16488". Thus, the name would be tagged :
    <rilmPerson xml:id="id-d70f1dc4-4171-11ec-81d3-0242ac130003" key="16488"><hi rend="bold"><persName><surname>Schönbach</surname> <forename>Dieter</forename></persName></hi>...
    *NEW 1/1/2022*: If a name cannot be matched with those in the list provided by RILM, include an empty @key attribute (key='').
  • Typography: render all single quotes as ’ (hex2019); double quotes (« ») as <q> tags (except in bibliographies; see below).
  • Typography: normalize case where small caps are used in the print (particularly in bibliographies).
  • Typography: superscript "o" used as an ordinal (as in "50º", meaning "50th") should be encoded as hex00ba ("masculine ordinal"), not as hex00b0 ("degree sign"), nor as a <hi> element.
  • *NEW 3/1*: Images in Biografie are limited to family tree diagrams. These should be captured as PNG images. These will be referenced in the TEI in a <figure> element (with an @xml:id attribute), containing a <graphic> element with a @url attribute pointing to the image file.

3. Short & Medium person articles:

1-6 paragraphs of body text, followed by worklists and/or bibliographies in paragraph form.

  • The following data are given in the first sentence of short and medium articles, in this order: Surname, Forename(s). Occupation(s) nationality. (birth place name, dd-MM-yyyy - death place name, dd-MM-yyyy). Use the name in bold as the <title> in the TEI header. *NEW 2/14/2022* Begin the <body> with a <div type="subheading">.
    [Balfoort, Dirk Jacobus. Musicologo e violinista oland. (Utrecht, 19-VII-1886 - L’Aia, 11-XI-1964).]
    <div type="subheading">
    <p><rilmPerson><hi rend="bold"><persName><surname>Balfoort</surname>, <forename>Dirk</forename> <forename>Jacobus</forename></persName></hi>. <occupation>Musicologo</occupation> e <occupation>violinista</occupation> <nationality><abbr>oland.</abbr></nationality> (<birth><placeName>Utrecht</placeName>, <date>19-VII-1886</date></birth> - <death><placeName>L’Aia</placeName>, <date>11-XI-1964</date></death>)</rilmPerson>....</p></div>
    • If an additional name or names appears in italic, in parentheses immediately following the bold type or after the word "nata", tag it with <surname type="alt">:
      [Baliani, Carlo (Bagliani, Balliani, Basiliani).]
      <persName><hi rend="bold"><surname>Baliani</surname>, <forename>Carlo</forename></hi> (<surname type="alt">Bagliani</surname>, <surname type="alt">Balliani</surname>, <surname type="alt">Basiliani</surname>)</persName>
    • If an additional name or names appears in italic in parentheses after the word "detto", tag it with <forename type="alt">:
      [Corea, Armando Anthony (detto Chick)]
      <persName><hi rend="bold"><surname>Corea</surname>, <forename>Armando</forename> <forename>Anthony</forename></hi> (detto <forename type="alt">Chick</forename>)</persName>
    • If the additional name has a qualifier "prop.", "pseud. di", or "nome d’arte di", tag the italic name in a separate <persName> element with @type="alt" within the same <rilmPerson> element:
      [Colonne, Edouard (pseud. di Jules-Edouard Judas).]
      <rilmPerson><hi rend="bold"><persName><surname>Colonne</surname>, <forename>Edouard</forename></persName></hi> (pseud. di <hi rend="italic"><persName type="alt"><forename>Jules-Edouard</forename> <surname>Judas</surname></persName></hi>).
    • If a birth or death date is given as a year only, with no other qualifier such as "ca.", include @precision="medium" in the <date> tag.
    • If a date is given with a preceding "ca.", such as "ca. 1733", include @precision="low".
    • If any of the birth or death information is given as "?", then leave the affected element empty or with a "?", then include @precision="unknown" in the affected element:
      [(?, 1543 - Parma, 13-III-1627)]
      <birth><placeName precision="unknown">?</placeName>, <date presision="medium" when="1543" when-custom="1543">1543</date></birth> - <death><placeName>Parma</placeName>
    • If a range of dates is given for birth or death information, use @from and @to instead of @when, and also include @precision="low":
      [Dufay, Guillaume. Compositore franco-fiamm. (prob. Fayt, Hainaut, ca. 1390-1400 - Cambrai, 27-XI-1474).]
      <div type="subheading">
      <p><rilmPerson><persName><surname>Dufay</surname>, <forename>Guillaume</forename>. <occupation>Compositore</occupation> <nationality>franco-fiamm.</nationality> (<birth>prob. <placeName precision="low">Fay, Hainaut</placeName> <date from="1390" to="1400" precision="low">1390-1400</date></birth>
    • *NEW 2/14/2022* If the death place is given as "ivi", please replace this with the place given for the birth:
      [(Roma, 1821 - ivi, ottobre 1894)]
      <birth><placeName>Roma</placeName>, <date presision="medium" when="1821" when-custom="1821">1821</date></birth> - <death><placeName>Roma</placeName>, <date precision="medium" when="1894-10-01" when-custom="1894">ottobre 1894</date></death>
  • Worklists may be headed "Compos.", "Ediz.", or "Scritti".
    • *NEW 2/14/2022*: Wrap the entire worklist in a single <div type="works">.
    • Normalize the case of the heading, spell out "Composizioni" or "Edizioni", and tag as <head>.
    • Parse the run-on into a <list> using paren-semicolon-space "); " as the delimiter between <item>s.
    • Tag italic text as <title>.
    • *revised 1/2022*: Where the word "per" appears, tag the Roman text after "per" as <instrumentation>.
    • If there is Roman text between italics and the word "per", tag as <genre>
    • Tag text in parentheses as <placeName> (if given) and <date>
    • Omit the semicolon and final period.

    [COMPOS. (ord. cronol.): Les caprices de Marianne, mus. di scena (1960); Pour un dix août per 2 v. recit., TB, coro e orch. (1971); 4 essais pour une fête per 4 soli, coro e orch. (1973).]
    <div type="works">
    <head>Composizioni (ord. cronol.):</head>
    <list>
    <item><bibl><title>Les caprices de Marianne</title>, <genre>mus. di scena</genre> (<date>1960</date>)</bibl></item>
    <item><bibl><title>Pour un dix août</title> per <instrumentation>2 v. recit., TB, coro e orch.</instrumentation> (<date>1971</date>)</bibl></item>
    <item><bibl><title>4 essais pour une fête</title> per <instrumentation>4 soli, coro e orch.</instrumentation> (<date>1973</date>)</bibl></item>
    </list>
    </div>
    [SCRITTI: De hollandsche vioolmakers (Amsterdam; 1931); Eigenartige Musikinstrumente (L'Aia, 1932).]
    <div type="works">
    <head>Scritti:</head>
    <list>
    <item><bibl><title>De viool en hare meesters</title> (<placeName>L’Aia</placeName>, s. a.)</bibl></item> <item><bibl><title>De hollandsche vioolmakers</title> (<placeName>Amsterdam</placeName>, <date>1931</date>)</bibl></item>
    <item><bibl><title>Eigenartige Musikinstrumente</title> (<placeName>L’Aia</placeName>, <date>1932</date>)</bibl></item>
    </list>
    </div>

    Longer worklists will be divided into sections with heads that may begin a new line, or continue on the same line. The division between sections can be found with the pattern "). ", that is, closing paren-period-space. Start a new <div type="section"> here. Use the text up to the following colon as <head>:
    [...1916). Per orch.: 5 sinfonie...]
    ...<date>1916</date>).</bibl></item></list></div>
    <div type="section">
    <head>Per orch.:</head>
    <list><item><bibl>5 sinfonie...

    NB: Many worklists will end with a miscellaneous grouping of works indicated by "Inoltre", "Inoltra", or "altra". Again, use "); " to distinguish <items>, but do not tag as <bibl>. Please see the example files for this.
  • Bibliographies are headed "BIBL.:" The individual entries are separated by semicolon-space ("; ") just like the worklists.
    • Tag italic text as <title>
    • Note that when the subject's name appears in the title of a bibliographic reference, it will be abbreviated. They should be expanded.
    • Tag names before the title as <author> and <persName>
    • Tag text within angle quotation marks as <title type="periodical">
    • Tag info in parentheses as <placeName>, <date>, and/or <edition> as needed:
    From an article on "Štejnberg, Maksimilian":
    [BIBL.: A. N. RIMSKIJ-KORSAKOV, M. Š. Mosca, 1928; V. BOGDANOV-BERESOVSKIJ, M. Š., ivi, 1947; M. GNESIN, M. Š in «Sovetskaja Muzyka», 1946;…]
    <div type="ography">
    <head>Bibliography:</head>
    <list>
    <item><bibl><author><persName><forename><abbr>A.</abbr></forename> <forename><abbr>N.</abbr></forename> <surname>Rimskij-Korsakov</surname></persName></author>, <title>Maksimilian Štejnberg</title> <pubPlace>Mosca</pubPlace>, <date>1928</date></bibl></item>
    <item><bibl><author><persName><forename><abbr>V.</abbr></forename> <surname>Bogdanov-Beresovskij</surname></persName></author>, <title>Maksimilian Štejnberg</title>, <pubPlace>Mosca</pubPlace>, <date>1947</date></bibl></item>
    <item><bibl><author><persName><forename><abbr>M.</abbr></forename> <surname>Gnesin</surname></persName></author>, <title type="article">Maksimilian Štejnberg</title>, in <title type="periodical">Sovetskaja Muzyka</title>, <date>1946</date></bibl></item>
    </list>
    • N.B. 1: The indication "ivi" in bibliographies means "in the same place". It should be replaced with the value of the <pubPlace> element in the <bibl> immediately before. This is highlighted in the second <item> of the example above.
    • N.B. 2: In a bibliography (not elsewhere), double quotes (« ») around a phrase in Roman type identify it as a periodical title. set @type to "periodical", and the @type of the <title> before it to "article". These are also highlighted in the example above. If double quotes appear within Italic type, tag as <q>.
4. Family articles

These begin with the family surname, followed by the word "Famiglia". Then follow numbered articles--each follows the format of the short person articles, except that only forenames are given, and in italic. Compare:
- Short person article: [Schuman, William Howard. Compositore amer. (New York, 4-VIII-1910).]
- Short family article, person: [- 1) William Howard. Compositore amer. (New York, 4-VIII-1910).]

  • Use the bold family name as <title> in the TEI header.
  • Tag the first line(s) as <div type="subheading"><p>.
  • Tag the family surname as <rilmPerson xml:id="id-xxx"><persName><hi rend="bold"><surname>...
  • If one or more surname variants are given in italic, tag <surname type="alt">.
  • Tag the last word of the first <p> as <nationality>.
[Škroup (Skraup). Famiglia di musicisti boemi.]
<rilmPerson xml:id="id-000"><persName><hi rend="bold"><surname>Škroup</surname></hi> (<hi rend="italic"><surname type="alt">Skraup</surname></hi>)</persName>. Famiglia di musicisti <nationality>boemi</nationality>.</rilmPerson>
  • Wrap each of the individual person articles in a <div type="person">, and within it, <div type="bio">, <div type="works">, or <div type="ography"> as needed.
  • Add a @corresp element to the <rilmPerson> element pointing to the @xml:id of the <rilmPerson> containing the family name.
  • Treat worklists and bibliographies just as in stand-alone person articles.

    See example file "deu00003.xml".

  • *NEW 3/1/2022*: Many family articles will include a genealogical diagram. These should be captured as PNG images. Create a <div type="genealogy"> inside the <div type="subheading">. Reference the image file in a <figure> element (with an @xml:id attribute), containing a <graphic> element with a @url attribute containing the image file name.
5. Long person articles

These begin like short articles, but there will be a "SOMMARIO" section following the birth-death information. The sommario is a list of sections in the body text of the article, like a table of contents:
[Skrjabin, Aleksandr Nikolaevič (Skriabin). Compositore e pianista russo (Mosca, 25-XII-1871/6-I-1872 - ivi, 14/27-IV-1915). SOMMARIO: I. La vita, il pensiero. II. L’opera sinfonica. - III. Il pianoforte.…]

  • Tag the name, occupation, nationality, and birth and death data just like short person articles.
  • Omit the Sommario.
  • Tag the first <div> as @type="subheading", and subsequent sections (indicated by heads) as <div type="section">.
  • Worklists in long person articles are often a combination of tables and lists in paragraph form.
    • Such worklists are headed "CATALOGO DELLE OPERE", in small caps. Use this as <head> for the <div type="works">.
    • *NEW 2/14/2022*: Wrap the entire worklist in a single <div type="works">; sections within it tag as <div type="section">.
    • *NEW 2/14/2022*: In columns headed "Rappresentazione" ("performance"), the entire contents of each <cell> should be wrapped in a <rilmPerformance> element. Tag the word before the comma as <placeName>, after it as <orgName type="performing">, and tag the <date>.
    • *NEW 2/14/2022*: In columns headed "Edizione", tag the text before the comma as <publisher>, and the place name after the comma as <pubPlace>.
    • Many worklists in long person articles will include an introductory paragraph or paragraphs. Tag these as <p>, tag italics as <hi>, and replace double-quotes with <q> tags.
    • Each table or list will have its own heading in small caps. Begin a new <div type="section"> and use the text in small caps as <head>.
    • Tables vary in the number of columns and in column headings. Tag the first row as @role="label". Use "—" (hex 2014) where dashes indicate missing or repeated values.
    • Tables that span two or more pages will have "Segue:" ("continue") at the top of the page. Continue these in the same <table> element, and omit the "Segue" text. Do not repeat the label row of the table.
      See example file "deu00004.xml".
    • Worklists in paragraph form may come before, between, or after tables. Note that in example deu00004.pdf, the lists headed "MUSICA VOCALE: b)" actually begins on the page before the table headed "MUSICA VOCALE: a)"!
6. Corporate articles

Articles on corporations are included in the Biografie. The first sentence of the article states the role of the institution, and nationality:
[Bärenreiter-Verlag. Casa editrice mus. ted., con sede a Kassel e Basilea, fondata ad Auststa nel 1923 da Karl → VÖTTERLE.]

<orgName><hi rend="bold">Bärenreiter-Verlag</hi></orgName>. Casa editrice mus. ted., con sede a Kassel e Basilea, fondata ad Auststa nel 1923 da Karl <ref target="deu00000">Vötterle</ref>.

Tag only the corporate name as <orgName>. Treat ~ographies the same as in person and family articles. (see no. 8, below, about the arrow).

7. <docAuthor>s and editors

Many articles will have an author given after the end of the text, justified right in small caps. (See example files "deu00005"). Make a <div type="authors"> for this, and wrap the author's name in <docAuthor> and <persName> with tags for <forename> and <surname>.

<div type="authors"><persName><forename>Paulheinz</forename> <surname>Beck</surname></persName></div>

Frequently, a translator's name will also be given, on the same line, justified left in normal case, in parens, preceded by "trad.":
[(trad. Vera Bertolino)]
Place these in the same <div type="authors">, but in a separate <docAuthor> element. Add an @type attribute with value "translator" to the <docAuthor>.

<docAuthor role="translator">(trad. <persName><forename>Vera</forename> <surname>Bertolino</surname></persName>)</docAuthor>
  • NB: Although the translator's name appears first in the print, reading left to right, include it in the TEI after the true author.
  • In rare cases, there will be more than one true author in small caps. Again, tag the two authors' names in the same <div> but separate <docAuthor>s.
  • In rarer cases, there may be two true authors and a translator.
  • As an extreme example, this appears in vol. II:
    (trad. Velia Donadei-Giacosa) NORBERT DUFOURCQ e RÉMY STRICKER
    (catalogo e bibl. a cura della Redazione)
    In this case, you would tag the two authors, then the translator, then add the note in a <p> element, still inside the same <div type="authors">.
    <div type="authors"> <docAuthor><persName><forename>Norbert</forename> <surname>Dufourcq</surname></persName></docAuthor>
    <docAuthor><persName><forename>Rémy</forename> <surname>Stricker</surname></persName></docAuthor>
    <docAuthor role="translator">(trad. <persName><forename>Velia</forename> <surname>Donadei-Giacosa</surname></persName>)</docAuthor>
    <p>(catalogo e bibl. a cura della Redazione)</p>
    </div>
7. See-references

An article heading followed by a right-pointing arrow (→), followed by text in small caps, indicates a see-reference:
[De Ana, Francesco → ANA, FRANCESCO D’.]
Do not make an XML file or PDF for these, but treat them as described at http://rilm.info/ft/tei_rilm_instructions.html#see-refs

8. Cross-references
  • A right-pointing arrow (→) or by a wavy right-pointing arrow (⤳) within the body text indicates a cross reference, or see-also reference.
  • A straight arrow indicates a target within the same part of the encyclopedia—from Biografie to Biografie, for example.
  • A wavy arrow indicates a target in a different part of the encyclopedia—from Biografie to Lessico, for example.
  • They might point to a whole article, or to a section within an article:
    (⤳ DULCE MELOS) or (→ HOMER, 2)
    The first points to the whole "Dulce Melos" article in the Lessico part of the encyclopedia. The second points to person number 2 in the family article on "Homer" in the Biografie part.
  • *NEW 1/1/2022*: The text "cfr. §" indicates a reference to another section within the same article: (cfr. § Musica vocale, a). The text following "§" gives the <head> and head:@n of the <div> that is targeted. There are examples in the file deu00004.xml.

Tag a reference to a whole article as a <ref> element with @target containing the value of the @xml:id of the <text> element of the target article. Tag a reference to a section with @target containing the concatenated @xml:id of the <text> target, "#", and the @xml:id of the <div>:

<ref target="deu00000">Dulce Melos</ref>
<ref target="deu11111#id-9b05e5d0-5cf9-11ec-bf63-0242ac130002">Homer, 2</ref>

NB. Omit the arrow character from the TEI; the link created by the <ref> tag will mark the reference.

NB 2. Because we must convert the Biografie part first, the @target references to other parts of the encyclopedia must be given a placeholder value.

  • Apart from the front matter capture noted above, articles in the 1990 Appendice volume should be treated exactly as the ones in the eight main volumes.
  • The Appendice volume ends with "Musicisti e interpreti deceduti nel corso della pubblicazione dell’opera", a list of persons who died after the main volumes were published, giving name, and death data.
    [Agosti, Guido (pp. 27-28): Milano, 2-VI-1980]
    This information should be added to the Agosti article from Biografie vol. 1. The original reads:
    [Agosti, Guido. Pianista ital. (Forlì, 11-VIII-1901).]

    [Agosti, Guido. Pianista ital. (Forlì, 11-VIII-1901 - Milano, 2-VI-1980).

Appendice 2005

The second Appendice volume, titled Appendice 2005, consists of two parts:

  1. The first part contains both supplements to articles published in the Biografie volumes (including the first Appendice) and entirely new articles.
    • Supplements are indicated by a volume and page number reference in brackets, immediately following the article heading, such as
      Austin, William Weaver [I: 174].
      or, in family articles, after the individual member's name:
      - 2) Massimo [I: 84].
      Treat exactly like articles in the main volumes, except add the phrase " [supplement] to the <title> element in the TEI header.
      ...<titleStmt><title>Austin, William Weaver [supplement]</title>...
    • New articles do not have brackets following the heading, and thus are formatted just like other articles in the Biografie volumes and first Appendice volume. They should be tagged the same as those.
  2. *NEW 2/15/2022*: The second part, subtitled Repertorio delle voci biografiche del DEUMM ordinate per categoria, contains a series of name indexes organized by occupation. For now, tag each index as a <div type="section"> and as a <list>. Tag the name as <persName> with <forename> and <surname> as appropriate.
