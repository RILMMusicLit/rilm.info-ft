Algemene Muziek Encyclopedie

(amended 5/28/2015)

  1. This encyclopedia was published over a period of 5 years between 1979 and 1984. Each volume will have a different ISBN in the TEI headers. The <author> element in the TEI header will differ for volume 1:
    • Volume 1 is by J. Robijns and J. M. Mostaert
    • Volumes 2 through 9 are by J. Robijns and Miep Zijlstra
  2. Front Matter will include the title page, copyright page, and "Ten geleide".
  3. English-style single quotation marks (‘ and ’) are used throughout. This means that the same character is used for right quote and for apostrophe, and they have to be distinguished:
    • [...‘Grove’s Dictionary of Music and Musicians’...]
      <p>...<q>Grove’s Dictionary of Music and Musicians</q>...</p>
  4. Entries may contain the following six <div> @type values:
    • heading (bold type, on its own line in the original)
    • subheading (line following in parentheses, but no text out of the parentheses)
    • body
    • ography (optional)
    • works (optional)
  5. Person articles give the name in a heading in surname, forename(s) order, where the surname is set in Bold, all-caps, and the forenames in Bold, normal case.
    • Alternate names appear in the heading in parentheses.
    • The subheading starts a new line with an open parenthesis mark, and contains places and dates. Birth information is marked with °, death information with †. Known birth or death dates are given in d.m.yyyy format. Sometimes only a year or century ("eeuw") is given. Others merit only a question mark:
      • [AARBURG, Ursula
        (° Lübeck 13.1.1924 - † Bremen 15.4.1967)]
      • [ABONDANTE, Giulio
        (16de eeuw)]
      • [ABRAMS, Harriet
        (° 1760 - † ?)
      Set the contents of the parenthesis in a <rilmPerson> element pointing to the heading, and tag the information presented. Omit the ° and † characters.
      <div type="heading" xml:id="d1">
      <p><rilmPerson xml:id="r1"><persName><surname>AARBURG</surname>, <forename>Ursula</forename></persName></rilmPerson></p>
      </div>
      <div type="subheading" xml:id="d2">
      <p><rilmPerson xml:id="r2" corresp="#r1">(<birth><placeName>Lübeck</placeName> <date when="1924-01-13" when-custom="1924" >13.1.1924</date></birth> - <death><placeName>Bremen</placeName> <date when="1967-04-15" when-custom="1967" >15.4.1967</date></death>)</rilmPerson></p>
      </div>
    • The first sentence of the body begins with the person's nationality, a space-hyphen-space, and the person's occupation(s). Set up to the first period or semicolon of the first <p> in <div type="body"> in another <rilmPerson> element with a @corresp pointing to the <rilmPerson> element in the <div type="heading">. Note that there may be more than one nationality given.
      • AALTONEN, Erik Verner (Erkki)
        (° Hämeenlinna, Tavastikus 17.8.1910)
        Fin - Componist en dirigent, studeerde viool en compositieleer aan de Sibelius­Academie en voltooide zijn studie met bet examen voor orkestdirectie in 1947. Hij werkte als violist in verschillende orkesten en boekte groot succes met de Hiroshima Symfonie in 1949.
      <div type="heading" xml:id="d1">
      <p><rilmPerson xml:id="r1"><persName><surname>AALTONEN</surname>, <forename>Erik</forename> <forename>Verner</forename> (<forename type="alt" >Erkki</forename>)</persName></rilmPerson></p>
      </div>
      <div type="subheading" xml:id="d2">
      <p><rilmPerson xml:id="r2" corresp="#r1">(<birth><placeName>Hämeenlinna</placeName>, <placeName>Tavastikus</placeName> <date when="1910-08-17" when-custom="1910" >17.8.1910</date></birth>)</rilmPerson></p>
      </div>
      <div type="body" xml:id="d3">
      <p><rilmPerson xml:id="r3" corresp="#r1"><nationality>Fin</nationality> - <occupation>Componist</occupation> en <occupation>dirigent</occupation>, studeerde viool en compositieleer aan de Sibelius­ Academie en voltooide zijn studie met bet examen voor orkestdirectie in 1947</rilmPerson>. Hij werkte als violist in verschillende orkesten en boekte groot succes met de <hi rend="italic">Hiroshima Symfonie</hi> in 1949.</p> </div>
      A list of occupations in Dutch is below.
    • All italicized type within the body text, worklists, or ographies of a <text type="person"> article indicates a title, and should be tagged <title>. However, figure captions are generally set in italic type, so do not tag them as such.
    • Worklists
      • Worklists in this publication have headings in all caps, not bold, reading "WERKEN", "VOORNAAMSTE WERKEN", "COMPOSITIES", "GESCHRIFTEN", or "PUBLIKATIES"; all are <div type="works">. Note: headings "INSTRUMENTALE WERKEN" and "VOCALE WERKEN" do not indicate worklists.
      • Worklists are usually divided with Roman headings at the beginning of a line followed by a colon: "Piano:", "Kamermuziek:", "Orgel:" "Orkestwerken:". Begin a new <div type="section"> at these points. Individual items are denoted by either a comma or semicolon following a closing parentheses. Remember to omit the comma or semicolon in the tagged text. Tag any italic text as <title>:

        [VOORNAAMSTE WERKEN
        Piano
        Sonate in C, op. 1 (1853); Sonate in fis, op. 2 (1853); Sonate in f, op. 5 (1854); ... ]
        <div type="works"><head>VOORNAAMSTE WERKEN<head>
        <div type="section"><head>Piano</head>
        <list><item><bibl>Sonate in C, op. 1 (<date>1853</date>)</bibl></item>
        <item><bibl>Sonate in fis, op. 2 (<date>1853</date>)</bibl></item>
        <item><bibl>Sonate in f, op. 5 (<date>1854</date>)</bibl></item> ...</list>
        </div>
        </div>
        See the file alg-example-1.xml for further examples.
      • Note: Some worklists have extensive commentary in prose, which may make it difficult or impossible to parse out individual items. These should still be set in a <div type="works"> if one of the headings above (WERKEN, etc.) is encountered. Anything that can't be arranged into a list may be enclosed in <p> tags, but italic text can still be tagged <title>. See the file alg-example-2.xml.
      • Note 2: A few long articles contain a worklist in the middle of the article, followed by additional prose. An example is the "Mozart" article (vol. 7, pp. 16-20 are the worklist; bibliography begins on p. 24).
    • Bibliographies are headed BIBLIOGRAPHIE and are <div type="ography">. They are in paragraph form with items separated by semicolons. Individual entries give author in surname, first initial form; the title in italic type; and one or more years.
      • Marix, J., Les musiciens de la cour de Bourgogne au XVe siecle, 1937; Id., Histoire de la musique et des musiciens de la cour de Bourgogne sous le regne de Philippe le Bon, 1939; Borren, Ch. van den, Etudes sur le XVe siecle musical, 1941;...
      <list><item><bibl><author><surname>Marix</surname>, <forename><abbr>J.</abbr></forename>, <title>Les musiciens de la cour de Bourgogne au XVe siecle</title>, <date>1937</date></bibl>;</item> <item><bibl><author>Id.</author>, <title>Histoire de la musique et des musiciens de la cour de Bourgogne sous le regne de Philippe le Bon</title>, <date>1939</date></bibl>;</item> <item><author><surname>Borren</surname>, <forename><abbr>Ch.</abbr></forename> <nameLink>van den</nameLink>, <title>Etudes sur le XVe siecle musical</title>, <date>1941</date></bibl>;</item> ...</list>
  6. Topic articles have headings in Bold, all caps (no normal case). Set the heading in <div type="heading">, <p>, and <term> tags.
    • Many topic articles begin the body with parenthesis. These may contain the language of the term in the heading and or translations of the term in the form (Lang.: term; Lang.: term), where a colon follows the abbreviated form of the language, and a semicolon separates one translation from the next.
      • BORING
        (Fr.: perce)
      • MOL
        (Du.: Be of B; Eng: flat; Fr.: bémol; It.: bemolle)
      Tag the term between the colon and semicolon as <term> with an @xml:lang attribute corresponding to the abbreviation given:
      • Du. = de
      • Fr. = fr
      • It. = it
      • En. = en
      <div type="heading"><p><term xml:id="t1">MOL</term></p></div>
      <div type="subheading"><p>(Du.: <term xml:id="t2" xml:lang="de" corresp="#t1">Be of B</term>; Eng: <term xml:id="t3" xml:lang="en" corresp="#t1">flat</term>; Fr.: <term xml:id="t4" xml:lang="fr" corresp="#t1">bémol</term>; It.: <term xml:id="t5" xml:lang="it" corresp="#t1">bemolle</term>)</p></div>
    • Italic type within <text type="topic"> article should not be tagged <title>, but should be tagged <hi rend="italic">
  7. See references, both stand-alone and within body texts, are indicated by a right-arrow (→)
    • Treat stand-alone see references according to the general rules for see references.
    • References within articles are presented the same way. Tag as <ref> elements.
    • Omit the arrows.
  8. Volume 10 contains a list of contributors (Medewerkers...). Tag as <text type="matter"> and set as a <list>. Each <item> contains a <rilmPerson> with a personal name, occupation, and place; and often an organization name.
    • The personal name lies on its own line. Any lower-case letters immediately after the comma are role names. Lower-case words at the end are Link names.
    • Occupation is given at the beginning of the next line in all lower-case characters. If there is more than one occupation, they are separated by comma.
    • The organization name, if present, follows without any punctuation, but with upper-case initials.
    • There is a semicolon before the place name.
    Examples:
    • Abeele, lic. G. van den
      oud-muziekpedagoog; Lederberg-Gent
      <item><rilmPerson><persName><surname>Abeele</surname>, <roleName>lic.</roleName> <forename><abbr>G.</abbr></forename> <nameLink>van den</nameLink></persName> <occupation>oud-muziekpedagoog</occupation>; <placeName>Lederberg-Gent</placeName></rilmPerson></item>
    • Ackere, J. van
      musicoloog, hoogleraar Universitaire Faculteiten St.-Ignatius Anterwerpen; Brussel
      <item><rilmPerson><persName><surname>Ackere</surname>, <forename><abbr>J.</abbr></forename> <nameLink>van</nameLink> <occupation>musicoloog</occupation>, <occupation>hoogleraar</occupation> <orgName>Universitaire Faculteiten St.-Ignatius Anterwerpen</orgName>; <placeName>Brussel</placeName></rilmPerson></item>
    • Albert, K.
      componist, oud-adjunct-directeur muziekdienst B.R.T.; Antwerpen
      <item><rilmPerson><persName><surname>Albert</surname>, <forename><abbr>K.</abbr></forename></persName> <occupation>componist</occupation>, <occupation>oud-adjunct-directeur muziekdienst</occupation> <orgName>B.R.T.</orgName>; <placeName>Antwerpen</placeName></rilmPerson></item>
  9. (new 5/28/15) The Afkortingen (abbreviations) section may be set as one file, <text type="matter"> with six sections.
    • Section 1 (.. van het notenschrift) can be set as paragraphs (<p> elements).
    • Section 2 (.. van muziektermen) should be set as a <table> with two columns.
    • Section 3 (.. van instrumentnamen) includes a long introductory paragraph (<p>), followed by a <table> with two columns, with one row for each abbreviation in the left column. Note that each row in the left column begins with a capital letter. Thus, "Ob – heck" constitutes one row.
    • Section 4 (.. van muziektijdschriften ..) includes an introductory paragraph and a two-column table. Tag italic text as <title>.
    • Sections 5 AND 6 are bibliographies. Unfortunately, the abbreviations are not in their own columns here, so set these as <list> elements containing <item> / <bibl>. Set italic text as <title>. Don't tag other elements within <bibl>.
  10. (new 5/28/15) The Termen section should be set as one file, <text type="matter"> with an introductory <p> and a <table> with five columns. Make one row for each term in the left-most column. Don't make a new row for hanging indented terms: "Acc. =" is the beginning of one row, "Accarezzevole" is the next, "Accel. =" is the following. Do not mark the italic text as <hi> or as <title>.
  11. (new 5/28/15) The Nederlands-Italien section should be set in one file, as a <table> with two columns; italic type in the left column, Roman in the right. Make a row for each term in the left column. Each begins with a capital letter, thus "Aan de rand van het membraan" is one term. Do not mark up the italic text as <hi> or as <title>.
  12. (new 5/28/15) The Muziekinstrumenten section should be set as one file, <text type="matter">, as a <table> with five columns. Make one row for each term in the left-most column. Don't make a new row for hanging indented terms. Do not mark the italic text as <hi> or as <title>.
  13. (new 5/28/15) The Correcties en aanvullingen (corrections and additions) section should be set as one file, <text type="matter"> with an introductory <p> followed by a <list>. Make an <item> for each entry beginning with bold-face type. Where possible, make a <ref> for each entry, pointing to the file with that title.
