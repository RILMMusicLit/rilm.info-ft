Biographical Dictionary of Musicians
  1. This publication should be converted from scanned print.
  2. General comments:
    • All entries are <text type="person">
    • Entries may contain any or all of the following sections, always in this order:
      • Name
      • Biographical facts and figures in chronological order
      • worklist
      • essay
      • bibliography
      All four should be sibling <div> elements.
    • Worklists and bibliographies may be divided into sections, marked by words in italic type.
    • Double quotes ( “ ” ) indicate titles throughout.
  3. Front matter will include the original title page (the one that carries the date 1886); Preface; and list of Abbreviations.
  4. Entries are headed with the name form "SURNAME (Forename Forename NameLink)." For the <title> element in the TEI header, omit the parentheses and add a comma after the surname (SURNAME, Forename NameLink). For the <body>, keep the parentheses as in the original. See the example files.
    • Wrap the entire contents of the first paragraph in <rilmPerson> tags.
    • The name is followed immediately by nationality and occupation.
    • Birth and death information are indicated by "B." and "D." The death information comes at the end of the paragraph.
  5. Worklists are headed with the work "WORKS." Omit the m-dash.
    • Items in the worklist are inline (no line breaks between), and are delimited by period or by semicolon.
    • Worklists may be divided into sections, according to category. These sections have inline heads in italic type, such as Operas: or Songs:
  6. See references are in the form [ZOTTI (Carlo). See CROAL (George).] Handle according to the general instructions.
  7. Back matter includes Appendix of Additions and Corrections; and a Bibliographical Subject-Index. Set both as lists. Make a new <div> for each section in the bibliography with a Bold heading.

Please refer also to sample files.
