Dizionario e bibliografia della musica (1836)

This publication should be converted from rich PDF.

The first two volumes comprise the Dictionary ("Dizionario"); the second two volumes (3-4) comprise the bibliography ("bibliografia"). They are essentially separate publications.

  • Dizionario:
    1. Front matter includes only a title page and a Prefazione (preface)
    2. All entries are <text type="topic">
    3. Entries have only <div type="body">. There are no headings or lists
    4. Footnotes should be handled as described in the general instructions.
    5. The article title is given in all-capital type. Start with a <div type="body"> and <p>. Use the word or words in all capitals as the <title> in the TEI header and tag it <term> within the <p>.
    6. See references and Cross references are indicated by an italic letter "V" followed by a period.
      • The "v" may be capital or lower case.
      • The target term is set in small capitals.
      • Examples:
        • [A CAPRICCIO, ad arbitrio dell'esecutore. V. AD LIBITUM.]
        • [Il sig. Mott a Londra, aggiunse recentemente a' Pianoforti un meccanismo simile del sig. Walker (v. CELESTINO), e ne rese il suono più forte ancora.]
      • Treat stand-alone See references according to the general rules for see references. If there is text between the referring term and the target term, include it in the <term> element in the target article.
      • For Cross references within articles, tag only the text in small caps (not the word "articolo", for example).
      • If the letter "v" is not italic / slanted, the reference is to another publication.
      • There are examples of multiple referring terms pointing to a single target term by way of a large brace "}". There are examples in vol. 1, on pages 13 and 23 (among many others).
    7. There are illustrations within some articles, but all musical examples (notated music) are printed together in volume 2, beginning after page 301. The are referred to in the articles as "Fig.1", "Fig.2", etc. Please separate these figures each in their own image file. and reference them in the appropriate articles.
    8. Set the articles in the Aggiunte section identically to others in the Dictionary.
    9. Set the Errata list as a <table>.
  • Bibliografia
    1. The front matter includes a preface (Prefazione) and a table of contents (Tavola della materie).
    2. Quotation marks are low and double for both opening and closing: („ text „).
    3. Please see the file DBM-TOC.pdf. In principle, make each item in the table of contents into a separate file IF it is more than just a heading. Parts outlined in the file should be marked up in one file.
    4. Mark up prose passages as body text, but tag italic type as <title> elements:
      • [g)
        ARISTIDE QUINTILIANO
        vivea secondo Fabricìo dopo Cicerone (vale a dire verso il principio dell'Era cristiana), avendolo criticato nel suo secondo libro De musica y p. 69, per avere scritto contro la musica nella sua Opera De repubblica...]
      <div type="section" n="g)"><head>ARISTIDE QUINTILIANO</head> <p>vivea secondo Fabricìo dopo Cicerone (vale a dire verso il principio dell'Era cristiana), avendolo criticato nel suo secondo libro <title>De musica</title> y p. 69, per avere scritto contro la musica nella sua Opera <title>De repubblica</title>...</p></div>
      Enclose passages in smaller type in <note> tags.
    5. List entries are identifiable by either an all-capital name or italic title at the beginning. They may include any or all of the following:
      • The author name in surname-forename order, typeset with Surname in all capitals, and forename(s) in small-captitals in parentheses, followed by a comma:
        [KEMPELN (WOLFGANG VON),]
      • A biographical note, often containing birth and death information ("nato a" = "born at"; "morto a" = "died at"), followed by a colon:
        [I. R. consigliere della cancelleria aulica a Vienna, nato a Presburgo l’anno 1729, morto a Vienna nel 1804:]
      • A title, in italic type, followed by a period:
        [Mechanismus der menschlichen Sprache, nebst der Beschreibung einer sprechenden Maschine, Mit 27 Kupfertafeln.]
      • Place and date of publication: [Wien, 1791.]
      • Other bibliographic information, possibly including other titles:
        [Anche in lingua francese col titolo: Le Mécanisme de la parole y suivi de la déscription d’une machine parlante, et enrichi de 27 planches. Ibid.]
      • An annotation, usually in smaller type:
        [Nella sua traduzione tedesca delle Malattie nervose del Tissot, 1’Autore parla a lungo della caratteristica della voce cantante, e per estratto nella musicale Gazzetta reale di Spira, 1788.]

    6. When an author name is printed in all-caps with no small caps, enclose the name in <author> tags but omit <forename> and <surname> tags: [ERODOTO D' ALICARNASSO]; [TEONE DI SMIRNA]
    7. Entries without author begin with a title in italic type.
    8. Some "lists" may contain only one item!
