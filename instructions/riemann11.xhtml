Hugo Riemanns Musik Lexikon - Elfte Auflage (1929) (rmn)

(revised 2015/5/6)

  1. There are two types of articles: <text type="person"> and <text type="topic">. Both types begin with a word in boldface followed by a comma. Articles about persons can be distinguished by:
    1. the presence in the first sentence of birth and/or death information, signalled by "*" and/or "†"; OR
    2. the presence in the first sentence of an occupation name from this list
  2. For articles about a person, tag the entire first sentence of the article with <rilmPerson>. Within that, the name in bold, together with comma that follows it and the name that follows the comma, should be tagged <persName> (and <surname> and <forename> within that). If "*" or "†" appear, replace them, together with the following items that follow with <birth> or <death>: date, and a place preceded by either the word "zu" or the word "in", and add @when and @when-custom attributes to those dates.
    * 5. Aug. 1623 zu Arezzo, † 14. Okt. 1669 in Florenz;
    <birth><date when="1623-08-05" when-custom="1623">5. Aug. 1623</date> zu <place>Arezzo</place></birth>, <death><date when="1669-10-14" when-custom="1669">14. Okt. 1669</date> in <place>Florenz</place></death>;
    The place will always be what appears between "zu" or "in" and the next comma, semi-colon, or period.

    </date> zu <placeName>München</placeName></rilmPerson>,

  3. Most articles about persons include a worklist. These are indicated in the text by "~werke:" There may be a prefix ("Orchesterwerke:", "Chorwerke:", "Instrumentalwerke:") but never a suffix.
    • Set as a <div type="works">. Tag the word ending in "werke:" as a <head>
    • Each <item> is delimited by a semicolon. Tag as item/bibl, and omit the semicolons:

      Werke: Lieder op. 1, 2a, 5 (Rilkelieder), 8; Klavierstücke op. 2b, 4, 6, 7; Stück F moll für Klavier und Violoncell; weitere Klavierstücke, darunter Passacaglia As moll (1913); drei 6st. gemischte Chöre (1912).
      <div type="works"><head>Werke:</head> <list>
      <item><bibl>Lieder op. <hi rend="italic">1, 2a, 5</hi> (Rilkelieder), <hi rend="italic">8</hi></bibl></item>
      <item><bibl>Klavierstücke op. <hi rend="italic">2b, 4, 6, 7</hi></bibl></item>
      <item><bibl>Stück F <hi rend="italic">moll</hi> für Klavier und Violoncell</bibl></item>
      <item><bibl>weitere Klavierstücke, darunter Passacaglia <hi rend="italic">As moll</hi> (1913)</bibl></item>
      <item><bibl>drei 6st. gemischte Chöre (1912)</bibl></item></list></div>
  4. Words that follow the keyword "spr." within parentheses are pronunciations and use special characters outside the usual German alphabet. I'll give the combining variants of the symbols here.
    • a macron (U+0304)
    • a breve (U+0306)
    • an apostrophe (U+02BC)
    • an acute accent (U+0301)
    • a diaeresis and an acute accent together
    • a diaeresis and a macron together
    • a diaeresis and a breve together
    • an inverted breve over two or more letters (see #6 below)
    • a light diagonal strikethrough through one or more letters (see #6 below)
  5. For the inverted breve in pronunciations, there are two methods for representing this character. First, for six combinations that will be declared in the header, use a <g> tag in the following way (from page 2102):
