A Dictionary of the Avant-Gardes
  1. General comments:
    • This publication should be converted from the provided XML.
    • Except for the front matter, each <sec disp-level="B"> element in the source will correspond to one TEI file.
    • Values for @type in <text> element as follows:
      • Entry titles that contain a comma and lower-case text are @type="person".
      • For entry titles that are in all-caps, refer to the table below.
      • If the title neither has lower-case text nor is in the table, then @type="topic".
    • All <xref ref-type="page"> elements should be converted to the TEI element <pb>:
      <xref ref-type="page" id="page_443" />

      <pb n="443"/>
    • Convert all &-encoded characters to Unicode.
    • Convert all <italic> tags to <hi rend="italic">.
    • Ignore all <book-part-meta> elements and their contents.
    • Use American rule for single quotation marks.
  2. Front matter will include the title page, copyright page, Preface, and Introduction.
    • The title page will correspond with the source elements <book-title-group> and <contrib-group>.
    • The copyright page will correspond with the source <publisher>, <isbn>, <pub-date>, <permissions>, and <imprint-meta> elements.
    • The Preface begins with a <fig> and <disp-quote> element. Here and in the Introduction, tag the content of the source <disp-quote> with <cit rend="block"> and <quote> tags. The element <disp-quote> will be handled differently in the individual entries.
    • Re-tag the <sig-block> contents as <byline>.
    • The last <p> of the Introduction beginning with the text "Sources" is a bibliography. Tag as a <div type="ography">. Items are separated by semicolons. Tag as a <list>. Convert <italic> tags to <title> and mark up as many other elements as possible:
      [<p><bold>Sources</bold>: Kirby, Michael. <italic>The Art of Time</italic>. New York: Dutton, 1969; Kostelanetz, Richard, ed. <italic>Esthetics Contemporary</italic>. 2d ed. Buffalo, NY: Prometheus, 1989; Krantz, Stewart. <italic>Science and Technology in the Arts</italic>. New York: Van Nostrand Reinhold, 1974;...</p>]
      <div type="ography">
      <head>Sources:</head>
      <list>
      <item><bibl><author><surname>Kirby</surname>, <forename>Michael</forename></author>. <title>The Art of Time</title>. <pubPlace>New York</pubPlace>: <publisher>Dutton</publisher>, <date>1969</date></bibl></item>

  3. The titles of person entries give the name in surname-forename order.
    • For the <div type="heading">, tag the name as <rilmPerson> and <persName>. The part of the name in all-capitals is the <surname> and the others are <forename>.
    • If the <p> element immediately following the article title begins with an open parenthesis, it should be tagged <div type="subheading"> containing a <rilmPerson> with an @corresp attribute pointing to the xml:id of the <rilmPerson> element above it. Tag the birth and death dates.
      [<title>ACCONCI, Vito</title> <p>(25 January 1939).</p>]
      <div type="heading"><p><rilmPerson xml:id="r1"><persName><surname>ACCONCI</surname>, <forename>Vito</forename></persName></rilmPerson></p></div>
      <div type="subheading"><p>
      <rilmPerson xml:id="r2" corresp="#r1"> (<birth><date when="1939-01-25" when-custom="1939">25 January 1939</date></birth>)</rilmPerson></p>
      </div>
  4. Cross-references in-line within the article texts in the source XML have no markup other than that for small capital letters (<sc>). Set these references as a <ref> element targeting the approriate article. Then convert the letters inside the <sc> tags to lower case letters.
    [...a fascination with numbers reminiscent of R<sc>AYMOND</sc> Q<sc>UENEAU</sc>, though...]
    ...a fascination with numbers reminiscent of <ref target="#dag00000>Raymond Queneau</ref>, though...
  5. Most article have a worklist at the end. They are enclosed within <disp-quote> elements in the source XML. Tag the contents of <disp-quote> as <div type="works"> and tag each <p> within the <disp-quote> as a separate <div type="section">.
    • Use the contents of the <bold> elements as <head> in the TEI.
    • Items are separated only by a semicolon in the source.
