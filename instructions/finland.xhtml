Historical Dictionary of the Music and Musicians of Finland
  1. General comments:
    • This publication should be converted from scanned print
    • Entries will have <div type="body"> and one <div type="ography>.
    • American-style double quotation marks are used.
    • Italic type is used to indicate titles, so tag with <title> rather than <hi>.
  2. Front matter includes the Title page, copyright page, Contents, Acknowledgements, Introduction, Key to Finnish Pronunciation, and Key to Music Festivals.
  3. Person entries give
    1. the personal name given in surname, forename order, in all bold, all-capitals
    2. the person's birth and death date and place information, as available, in parentheses
    3. occupation
    Tag up to and including the occupation in a <rilmPerson> element (use the list of occupations here to match):
    [ACKTE, AINO (Apr. 23, 1876, Helsinki-Aug. 8, 1944, Nummela), a soprano, one of the most celebrated opera stars of her time, a pioneer of the Finnish opera, and voice teacher, daughter of opera singers Emmy and Lorenz Achte, sister of alto opera singer Irma Achte Tervani. ...]
    <p><rilmPerson><persName><surname>ACKTE</surname>, <forename>AINO</forename></persName> (<birth><date when="1876-04-23" when-custom="1876">Apr. 23, 1876</date>, <placeName>Helsinki</placeName></birth>–<death><date when="1944-08-08" when-custrom="1944">Aug. 8, 1944</date>, <placeName>Nummela</placeName></death>), a <occupation>soprano</occupation></rilmPerson>, one of the most celebrated opera stars of her time, a pioneer of the Finnish opera, and voice teacher, daughter of opera singers Emmy and Lorenz Achte, sister of alto opera singer Irma Achte Tervani. ...</p>
  4. Topic Articles have headings in bold, all-caps, with no internal comma: [JOENSUU SONG FESTIVAL].
    • Some topic headings have translations of a term in parentheses. [JOIKU (JOIK in English, JUOIGO in German), ..] In such cases, tag each of the bold, all-cap words as <term>:
      <p><term>JOIKU</term> (<term>JOIK</term> in English, <term>JUOIGO</term> in German), ...</p>
    • Terms or institution names in Finish are followed by an English translation in non-bold, normal case, in parentheses:
      [AKATEEMINEN LAULU (Academic Song)]
      [AKATEEMINEN MUSIIKKISEURA (Academic Music Society)]
      These may be institution names, titles, or events, so do not tag them as <term>.
  5. Bibliographies are headed in print with the word "Bibliography" in italics. Each item is separated with a line break and hanging indent.
    • The first author name is in surname-forename order. If there are additional authors, they are in forename-surname order.
    • Titles are in italic type; Article titles are in quotation marks.
    • Translations of Finnish titles are given in square brackets.
    [ Heiniö, Mikko, Pekka Jalkanen, Seija Lappalainen, and Erkki Salmenhaara. “Sarmanto, Heikki.” In Suomalaisia säveltäjiä [Finnish Composers]. Helsinki: Otava, 1994.
    Salminen, Anja. “Swept by Leino's Poems and Sarmanto's Music (Aina Swan Cutler).” Suomen silta [Suomi Bridge] (6/1995): 27–28.
    Suvanto, Antti. Heikki Sarmanto. Helsinki: Finnish Music Information Centre, 1989.]

  6. Cross references within articles are always in parentheses, and the target term is in all-capitals. [ (see TURKU MUSICAL SOCIETY) ] More than one reference may occur within one set of parentheses: [ (see DOMINICAN MONASTERIES, SAINT OLAF’S MONASTERY )] If the targets are persons, there will be a semicolon delimiting them: [ (see LITHANDER, CARL; LITHANDER, FREDRIK) ].

    • Appendices: A. Where is Finland? B. Chronology of Finnish History. C. Chronology of Finnish Music History. Set the chronologies as <list>s.
    • Selected Bibliography.
    • Index. Handle according to the general guidelines.
