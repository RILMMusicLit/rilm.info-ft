Hugo Riemanns Musik Lexikon - Elfte Auflage (1929) (rmn)

  1. Entries in Riemann have only one section each: <div type="body">.
  2. There are two types of articles: <text type="person"> and <text type="topic">. Both types begin with a word in boldface followed by a comma. Articles about persons can be distinguished by:
    1. a forename following that comma, followed by another comma; sometimes preceded a parenthetical statement, followed by a comma:
      <p><rilmPerson><persName><surname>Rogowski</surname>, <forename>Michael</forename><forename>Ludwig</forename></persName>,
      <p><rilmPerson><persName><surname>de Romain</surname> (spr. -ǟng&#x0361;), <forename>Louis</forename>,
    2. frequently, birth and/or death information, signalled by "*" and/or "†" in the first paragraph.
  3. For articles about a person, tag the entire first sentence of the article with <rilmPerson>. Within that, the name in bold, together with comma that follows it and the name that follows the comma, should be tagged <persName> (and <surname> and <forename> within that). If "*" or "†" appear, replace them, together with the following items that follow with <birth> or <death>: date, and a place preceded by either the word "zu" or the word "in", and add @when and @when-custom attributes to those dates.
    * 5. Aug. 1623 zu Arezzo, † 14. Okt. 1669 in Florenz;
    <birth><date when="1623-08-05" when-custom="1623">5. Aug. 1623</date> zu <place>Arezzo</place></birth>, <death><date when="1669-10-14" when-custom="1669">14. Okt. 1669</date> in <place>Florenz</place></death>;
    The place will always be what appears between "zu" or "in" and the next comma, semi-colon, or period.

    </date> zu <placeName>München</placeName></rilmPerson>,

  4. Articles that have neither a birth marker (*) nor a death marker (†) are topic articles.
  5. Words that follow the keyword "spr." within parentheses are pronunciations and use special characters outside the usual german alphabet. I'll give the combining variants of the symbols here.
    • a macron (U+0304)
    • a breve (U+0306)
    • an apostrophe (U+02BC)
    • an acute accent (U+0301)
    • a diaeresis and an acute accent together
    • a diaeresis and a macron together
    • a diaeresis and a breve together
    • an inverted breve over two or more letters (see #6 below)
    • a light diagonal strikethrough through one or more letters (see #6 below)
  6. For the inverted breve in pronunciations, there are two methods for representing this character. First, for six combinations that will be declared in the header, use a <g> tag in the following way (from page 2102):
    Maillard (spr. m<g ref="#aij-tie">aij</g>ăr)
