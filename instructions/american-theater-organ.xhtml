Encyclopedia of the American Theatre Organ

This publication is in three volumes. The first two were edited by David L. Junchen and published in 1985 and 1989. The third volume was edited by Preston J. Kaufmann and published in 1995.

  1. General comments:
    • This title should be converted from scanned print.
    • Entries can have <div type="heading">, <div type="body">, <div type="textBox>, <div type="works">.
    • Notes for all entries in a volume are printed at the end of each volume in a section called "Footnotes" (even though they are end notes).
    • In addition to the hundreds of photos, there are nearly as many facsimiles of documents, such as advertisements, catalog pages, and correspondence. These should be treated as images, even if they contain only text. Which are which should be clear because of different typefaces. If there is any question, please ask.
  2. Front matter for volume 1: frontispiece; title; copyright; dedication; foreword (Bowers); About Author; Acknowledgments; Author's Preface; Introduction to the American Theatre Organ.
    • Treat the first four pages of the PDF as advertisements.
    • The frontispiece contains only a photo and caption "The 6/51 Barton..." Use "[frontispiece]" (including the brackets) as the file title
    Front matter for volume 2: frontispiece; title page; copyright page; Acknowledgements.
  3. Entries for volumes 1 and 2 are listed in tables of contents at the beginning of each volume; entries themselves are separated in the pages by very heavy horizontal black rules. All entries are <text type="corporate">; except those listed below that are <text type="person">.
    • Headings in print are reproductions of corporate logos and / or letterhead. They must be treated as figures within the <div type="heading"> with a <figDesc> element containing the name of the firm or person.
    • "Opus List" sections at the ends of entries can be marked up as <div type="works">, but must be tagged as <table>, not as <list>.
    • Figure captions are printed in italic, with ROMAN used for titles and emphasis. [Schuelke ad in the September 1914 Diapason, the very month of the firm's bankruptcy.] Tag as a normal <p> element, and tag the Roman text as "italic".
      <p>Schuelke ad in the September 1914 <hi rend="italic">Diapason</hi>, the very month of the firm's bankruptcy.</p>
  4. Volume 3 contains much longer entries, all about one manufacturer. The table of contents for vol. 3 gives entry titles in all caps and section titles in normal case.
    • All entries are <text type="topic">.
    • Front matter for volume 3: frontispiece; title page; copyright page; dedication page; "CHARTS..." index; Author's Preface; Acknowledgments; Subscribers list.
    • Use the titles in all caps as a guide to creating files and file names for volume 3.
    • Use the section titles in normal case as a guide to tagging <div type="section"> elements and the contents of their <head> elements.
    • Whole-page boxed texts that are not listed in the table of contents--for example, "Capitalization & Other Financial Notes" on p. 25, should be treated as separate entries with their own files.
    • Biographical vignettes, such as "Franz Rudolph Wurlitzer" can be tagged as a <div type="textBox"> in the entry in which it appears, such as "The House of Wurlitzer."
    • Back matter for volume 3: Illustration acknowledgements; References (a bibliography in sections).
