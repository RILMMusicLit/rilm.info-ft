  3. The tags <xref ref-type="page"/> should be converted to the TEI element <pb> with @n containing the page number:
    <xref ref-type="page" id="page_2"/>

    <pb n="2"/>

The list of Contributing Authors beginning on page xx should be set as a series of lists, one <list> corresponding to each <sec> that has a <title> (Africa, etc.) Each item may be tagged with <rilmPerson>, and <affiliation> containing <persName>, <orgName> and <placeName>. Do not tag <forename> and <surname> because East-Asian names are given in different order.
    <p>Laura Arntson<break/>Public Health Institute,<break/>USAID Nigeria<break/>Abuja, Nigeria</p>
    <p>Fremont E. Besmer<break/>City College of the City University of New York<break/>New York, New York, U.S.A.</p>
    <item>
    <rilmPerson><persName>Laura Arntson</persName> <affiliation><orgName type="uni">Public Health Institute, USAID Nigeria</orgName> <placeName>Abuja, Nigeria</placeName></affiliation></rilmPerson>
    </item>
    <item>
    <rilmPerson><persName>Fremont E. Besmer</persName> <affiliation><orgName type="uni">City College of the City University of New York</orgName><placeName>New York, New York, U.S.A.</placeName></affiliation></rilmPerson>
    </item>
  5. The List of Reviewers beginning on page xxv should be treated the same.
  6. The table at the bottom of this page will be your guide to dividing this publication into separate files. The source XML is inconsistent in the way certain types of items are tagged and nested, so you will need to refer to the list.
    • Some articles are identifiable by a <book-part> element that has an @id attribute beginning "chapter". Others begin with a <sec> element. The table gives the chapter number and/or range of section numbers to include in each article.
    • Where the title in the list below and the contents of <title> in the source differ, please use the title in the list below.
    • All articles are <text type="topic"> and will have @head and @body. Not every article will include an @ography.
    The TEI samples for this publication show conversions from each style of source markup.
  7. Sections within an article--<sec> elements in the source xml beginning with a <title>--EXCEPT THOSE LISTED IN THE TABLE BELOW as starting an article--should begin a new <div type="section"> with a <head> element.
    <sec id="S627"> <title>Origins of <italic>Conjunto</italic> Music</title> <p>The beginnings of <italic>conjunto</italic> music can be traced...
    <div type="section"> <head>Origins of <hi rend="italic">Conjunto</hi> Music</head> <p>The beginnings of <hi rend="italic">conjunto</hi> music can be traced...</p></div>
  8. <boxed-text> elements in the source should be converted to a <div type="textBox">. If the <boxed-text> tag is followed by a <caption><title>, tag the contents of <title> as <head>.
  9. Quotations from song lyrics enclosed in a <verse-group> and <verse-line> elements should be placed within the preceding <p> element (if present; otherwise in its own), in <lg> tags, with each line in its own <l> tag:
    <verse-group>
    <verse-line>That which is Nyama,</verse-line>
    <verse-line>All things are hidden under Nyama,</verse-line>
    <verse-line>Nyama is not hidden under anything.</verse-line>
    </verse-group>
    <lg>
    <l>That which is Nyama,</l>
    <l>All things are hidden under Nyama,</l>
    <l>Nyama is not hidden under anything.</l>
    </lg>
  10. Author names come at the end of the body text in a <sig-block> element. Inside the nested <sig> element, there is an introductory phrase, such as
    <sig-block>
    <sig>
    <italic>—Adapted from an article by Laura Arntson</italic>
    </sig>
    </sig-block>
    Place these in a <byline> inside a <div type="authors">. Do not distinguish <forename> and <surname>, because East Asian names are given in a different order. Omit the <italic> tags:
    <div type="authors"><byline>—Adapted from an article by <docAuthor><persName>Laura Arntson</persName></docAuthor></byline></div>
  11. The <sc> tag is used in two contexts in this publication: for B.C.E. and C.E. indications and for "see" references to other articles in the publication.
    • See-references are in-line within the article texts and begin with a bracket: "[See ". These occur without any markup other than that for small capital letters (<sc>) within <p> elements in the source XML. Set these references as a <ref> element targeting the approriate article.
      <p>One type of music became popular all over southern Africa: the <italic>makwaya</italic>, from the English word <italic>choir</italic>. It involved singing, complex marching routines, and special costumes. Adaptations of jazz in responsorial form, accompanied by drumming and dancing, have become popular with young people. [See B<sc>LACK</sc> P<sc>OPULAR</sc> M<sc>USIC IN</sc> S<sc>OUTH</sc> A<sc>FRICA</sc>.]</p>
      <p>One type of music became popular all over southern Africa: the <hi rend="italic">makwaya</hi>, from the English word <hi rend="italic">choir</hi>. It involved singing, complex marching routines, and special costumes. Adaptations of jazz in responsorial form, accompanied by drumming and dancing, have become popular with young people. [See <ref target="cgw000??>Black Popular Music in South Africa</ref>.]</p>
      NB: One such reference points to two articles.
    • Convert the contents of all <sc> elements to lower-case letters, and then remove the <sc> tags. Then, change the text "b.c.e." and "c.e." to upper-case "B.C.E." and "C.E."
  12. Bibliographies in the source XML are tagged inconsistently. Sometimes they are a <sec> element with <title>Bibliography</title>, and sometimes they are a <ref-list> element inside a <back> element. Treat these equally as <div type="ography"> and tag the items according to the table for Taylor & Francis bibliographies on the main instruction page.
